## 2. Purpose

This policy sets out Crest Bank's approach to identifying, supporting, and treating fairly customers who are, or may be, in vulnerable circumstances. It establishes the standards and procedures that ensure vulnerable customers receive outcomes at least as good as those experienced by other customers, in line with Crest Bank's obligations under the Consumer Duty. This policy also provides staff with clear guidance on recognising vulnerability indicators, making reasonable adjustments, and recording relevant information appropriately.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Staff:** All Crest Bank employees and contractors who interact with customers directly or who design, deliver, or oversee customer-facing products and services. This includes Customer Operations, Product, Marketing, Technology, Compliance, and Risk functions.
- **Products and Services:** Personal current accounts, instant-access and fixed-term savings accounts, personal loans, and SME business current accounts.
- **Customer Segments:** All retail customers and SME customers, including prospective customers at the point of application.
- **Channels:** All digital channels through which Crest Bank serves customers, including the mobile application, web platform, in-app messaging, email, and telephone support.

## 5. Policy Statement

5.1 Crest Bank is committed to identifying customers who may be in vulnerable circumstances and ensuring they receive fair treatment, appropriate support, and outcomes equivalent to those received by non-vulnerable customers.

5.2 Crest Bank recognises that vulnerability is not a fixed state. It may be temporary, sporadic, or permanent, and a customer's circumstances may change over time. Crest Bank will treat vulnerability as a spectrum and will respond proportionately to the nature and severity of the circumstances identified.

5.3 Crest Bank will train all customer-facing staff, and relevant non-customer-facing staff, to recognise the four drivers of vulnerability — health, life events, resilience, and capability — and to respond with appropriate skill, sensitivity, and flexibility.

5.4 Crest Bank will make reasonable adjustments to its services, communications, and processes to ensure that vulnerable customers can access and benefit from its products and services on an equitable basis.

5.5 Crest Bank will record vulnerability flags on customer records in a consistent, proportionate, and privacy-respecting manner, and will use that information to inform subsequent interactions and product decisions.

5.6 Crest Bank will not design or market products in a way that exploits or takes advantage of customers in vulnerable circumstances. Product suitability assessments will explicitly consider the needs of potentially vulnerable customer groups.

5.7 Crest Bank will monitor outcomes for customers with vulnerability flags to identify and remediate any evidence of harm or differential treatment.

5.8 Crest Bank will review this policy at least annually and update it in response to changes in regulatory guidance, customer insight, or internal monitoring findings.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Identifying Vulnerability Indicators

In accordance with FCA Guidance FG21/1, Crest Bank recognises four primary drivers of vulnerability:

- **Health:** Physical or mental health conditions, including cognitive impairment, serious illness, addiction, or disability.
- **Life Events:** Bereavement, relationship breakdown, job loss, domestic abuse, or other significant life disruptions.
- **Resilience:** Low emotional resilience, difficulty coping with financial shocks, or susceptibility to pressure or influence.
- **Capability:** Low financial literacy, low digital literacy, language barriers, or limited experience managing financial products.

Staff will be alert to indicators of these drivers during all customer interactions, including in-app messaging, telephone calls, and written communications. Indicators may include: expressions of distress or confusion; requests for repeated explanations; disclosures of personal circumstances; unusual account activity suggestive of financial abuse; or third-party involvement in account management.

Staff must not make assumptions about vulnerability based on age, demographic characteristics, or product type alone.

### 7.2 Recording Vulnerability Flags

7.2.1 Where a staff member identifies or suspects a customer may be in vulnerable circumstances, they will record a vulnerability flag on the customer's record in Crest Bank's CRM system within one business day of the interaction.

7.2.2 The flag will include: the date of identification; the driver category (health, life event, resilience, or capability); a brief factual description of the circumstances disclosed or observed; and the adjustments made or offered.

7.2.3 Staff will record only information that is necessary and proportionate. In accordance with UK GDPR and guidance from the DPO, health-related information constitutes special category data and must be handled with additional care. Staff will not record diagnoses or clinical detail; they will record only the functional impact relevant to the customer's financial needs.

7.2.4 Vulnerability flags will be visible to authorised Customer Operations staff in subsequent interactions, so that customers are not required to repeat sensitive disclosures unnecessarily.

7.2.5 Customers will be informed that a note has been made on their account to help Crest Bank provide appropriate support, and will be advised of their right to request its removal.

### 7.3 Making Reasonable Adjustments

7.3.1 Upon identifying a vulnerability indicator, staff will consider and offer adjustments appropriate to the customer's circumstances. Adjustments may include, but are not limited to:

- Providing communications in alternative formats (e.g. larger text, simplified language, or audio).
- Allowing additional time during telephone interactions.
- Directing the customer to Crest Bank's dedicated vulnerability support line.
- Facilitating third-party account access under Crest Bank's Third Party Mandate procedure.
- Temporarily pausing collection activity or fee application where a customer discloses acute financial difficulty.
- Signposting to external support services (e.g. StepChange, Mind, Citizens Advice).

7.3.2 In accordance with PRIN 2A and BCOBS 5, all communications with customers who have an active vulnerability flag will be reviewed to ensure they are clear, appropriately toned, and do not create additional distress or confusion.

7.3.3 Staff will not pressure a vulnerable customer to complete a transaction or application where the customer has expressed uncertainty or distress.

### 7.4 Product Suitability and Design

7.4.1 In accordance with PRIN 12 and PRIN 2A, Product Managers will conduct a vulnerability impact assessment as part of the standard product approval and review process. This assessment will consider whether the product's features, terms, eligibility criteria, and associated communications are appropriate for customers who may be in vulnerable circumstances.

7.4.2 Where a product assessment identifies that a product or feature carries elevated risk of harm for vulnerable customers, the Product Governance Committee will determine whether design changes, additional safeguards, or targeted eligibility restrictions are required before the product is approved or continued.

7.4.3 Customer Operations staff will not recommend or facilitate the application of a product where they have identified a vulnerability indicator that makes the product clearly unsuitable for that customer's circumstances. Such cases will be escalated to a team leader for review.

## 8. Monitoring, Reporting and Escalation

8.1 The Head of Customer Operations will produce a monthly Vulnerability MI Report covering: the number of vulnerability flags recorded; flag categories; adjustments made; complaints received from flagged customers; and any identified patterns of poor outcome.

8.2 The Vulnerability MI Report will be reviewed at the monthly Compliance Oversight Committee, chaired by Rachel Whitfield.

8.3 The CCO will present a quarterly summary of vulnerability outcomes, including trend analysis and any identified systemic issues, to the Board Risk Committee, chaired by Michael Byrne.

8.4 Where a staff member identifies a case involving suspected financial abuse, coercion, or exploitation of a vulnerable customer, they will escalate immediately to their team leader and to the MLRO, James Patel, in accordance with Crest Bank's Financial Crime Policy.

8.5 Where monitoring identifies a pattern of poor outcomes for vulnerable customers attributable to a product, process, or system, the CCO will raise this as a priority item at the next Executive Risk Committee and will agree a remediation plan with the CRO within 20 business days.

---

## 9. Training

9.1 All customer-facing staff will complete mandatory vulnerability awareness training before commencing customer-facing duties and will refresh this training annually thereafter.

9.2 Training will cover: the four drivers of vulnerability; how to identify vulnerability indicators in digital and telephone interactions; how to record flags in the CRM system; how to make and document reasonable adjustments; and how to escalate complex or urgent cases.

9.3 Product Managers and Marketing staff will complete a supplementary module on vulnerability considerations in product design and communications, to be completed within 30 days of joining and refreshed every two years.

9.4 Training completion will be tracked by the Head of Customer Operations via Crest Bank's learning management system (LMS). Non-completion will be reported to the relevant line manager and escalated to the CCO if not resolved within 10 business days.

9.5 Training content will be reviewed annually by the CCO and updated to reflect changes in regulatory guidance, internal monitoring findings, or emerging vulnerability trends.

## 10. Review Cycle

10.1 This policy will be reviewed annually by the document owner, Priya Sharma, in consultation with the CCO and DPO, with any updates approved by the Compliance Oversight Committee.

10.2 An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- Material changes to FCA or PRA rules or guidance relating to vulnerable customers or Consumer Duty.
- A significant increase in vulnerability-related complaints or identified poor outcomes.
- A regulatory finding, skilled person review, or enforcement action relating to vulnerable customer treatment.
- A material change to Crest Bank's product range, customer base, or digital channels that affects the policy's scope.

10.3 All substantive amendments to this policy require approval by the Compliance Oversight Committee. Minor editorial corrections may be approved by the CCO and noted at the next Committee meeting.
