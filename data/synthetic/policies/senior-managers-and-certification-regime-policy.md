## 2. Purpose

This policy sets out Crest Bank Ltd's framework for complying with the Senior Managers and Certification Regime (SM&CR) as applied to dual-regulated firms by the Financial Conduct Authority and Prudential Regulation Authority. It establishes the obligations of Senior Managers, Certified Persons, and all other staff subject to the Conduct Rules, and defines the processes by which Crest Bank maintains accountability, assesses fitness and propriety, and fulfils its regulatory notification obligations. The policy exists to embed a culture of individual accountability throughout the organisation and to ensure that the right people hold the right responsibilities with appropriate oversight.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Senior Managers** holding a Senior Management Function (SMF) designation approved by the FCA or PRA;
- **Certified Persons** performing a Certification Function as defined under FCA rules;
- **All other Crest Bank employees**, in respect of the Conduct Rules applicable to them;
- All Crest Bank products and business lines, including personal current accounts, savings products, personal loans, and SME business current accounts;
- Contractors, consultants, and secondees who perform functions that would otherwise be subject to SM&CR obligations, where they meet the relevant regulatory definitions.

This policy does not apply to appointed representatives or third-party outsourcing partners, who are addressed separately in the Third-Party and Outsourcing Risk Policy.

## 4. Regulatory Context

| Regulatory Source | Relevance to This Policy |
|---|---|
| **FIT 1 and FIT 2** (FCA Handbook — Fit and Proper Test for Employees and Senior Personnel) | Sets the criteria — honesty and integrity, competence and capability, financial soundness — against which Senior Managers and Certified Persons must be assessed. |
| **SYSC 4.7** (FCA Handbook — Senior Management Arrangements, Systems and Controls) | Requires dual-regulated firms to allocate overall responsibility for each business area, activity, and management function to a Senior Manager, and to maintain accurate Statements of Responsibilities. |
| **SYSC 25** (FCA Handbook — Senior Managers and Certification Regime: Allocation of Responsibilities) | Sets out the prescribed responsibilities that must be allocated to named Senior Managers, including the requirement to maintain a Management Responsibilities Map. |
| **COCON** (FCA Handbook — Code of Conduct) | Specifies the individual Conduct Rules applicable to all staff (Tier 1) and the additional Senior Manager Conduct Rules (Tier 2), and requires Crest Bank to train staff on their obligations. |
| **SUP 10C** (FCA Handbook — FCA Senior Managers Regime: Approved Persons in Relevant Authorised Persons) | Governs the approval process for SMF holders, including application requirements, regulatory references, and notification of changes. |
| **PRA Rulebook — Senior Managers Regime** | Sets out PRA-specific SMF designations (e.g. SMF1 Chief Executive, SMF2 Chief Finance Officer) and the PRA's parallel fitness and propriety expectations for dual-regulated firms. |
| **SYSC 22** (FCA Handbook — Regulatory References) | Requires Crest Bank to obtain and provide regulatory references when appointing or terminating Senior Managers and Certified Persons. |

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank adopts the following principles in implementing the SM&CR:

1. **Individual accountability is foundational.** Crest Bank will at all times maintain a clear and complete allocation of responsibilities to named Senior Managers, ensuring there are no gaps or ambiguities in the Management Responsibilities Map.

2. **Senior Managers are personally responsible for their areas.** Each SMF holder accepts personal responsibility for the activities within their Statement of Responsibilities and will take reasonable steps to prevent regulatory breaches in their area, in accordance with the duty of responsibility under section 66A of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000.

3. **Fitness and propriety is a continuous obligation.** Crest Bank will assess the fitness and propriety of all Senior Managers and Certified Persons at appointment and at least annually thereafter, and will act promptly where concerns arise between scheduled assessments.

4. **Certification is a Crest Bank responsibility.** Crest Bank, not the regulator, is responsible for certifying that individuals performing Certification Functions are fit and proper. No individual will perform a Certification Function without a valid certificate issued by Crest Bank.

5. **Conduct Rules apply to all staff.** Every Crest Bank employee is subject to the Tier 1 Conduct Rules under COCON. Senior Managers are additionally subject to the Tier 2 Senior Manager Conduct Rules. Crest Bank will ensure all staff understand and comply with the rules applicable to them.

6. **Regulatory references will be obtained and provided honestly.** Crest Bank will obtain regulatory references for all Senior Manager and Certification Function appointments and will provide accurate, complete references when requested, in accordance with SYSC 22.

7. **Breaches will be reported promptly.** Crest Bank will notify the FCA and PRA of any changes to SMF holders, any conduct rule breaches by Senior Managers, and any matters requiring notification under SUP 10C without undue delay.

8. **A speak-up culture is actively maintained.** Crest Bank will not tolerate retaliation against any employee who raises concerns about SM&CR compliance in good faith.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Maintaining the Management Responsibilities Map

The CCO will maintain a current Management Responsibilities Map documenting all SMF holders, their designated functions, and the business areas, activities, and prescribed responsibilities allocated to each. In accordance with SYSC 25, the map will be reviewed and updated within 7 business days of any change to the senior management structure. The CCO will present a reviewed and confirmed map to the Board Risk Committee at each quarterly meeting.

### 7.2 Statements of Responsibilities

Each SMF holder will hold a current, signed Statement of Responsibilities (SoR) that accurately reflects their individual accountabilities. The CCO will issue an updated SoR to the relevant SMF holder within 10 business days of any material change to their role. SMF holders must review and countersign their SoR within 5 business days of receipt. Where an SMF holder disputes the allocation of a responsibility, they will escalate in writing to the CCO and, if unresolved, to the CEO within 5 business days. Signed SoRs will be retained by People & Culture for a minimum of 6 years following the individual's departure from the role.

### 7.3 FCA and PRA Approval of Senior Managers

Before any individual takes up an SMF role, the CCO will submit the relevant FCA Form A (or PRA equivalent) and supporting documentation, including a regulatory reference obtained in accordance with SYSC 22. No individual will perform an SMF without confirmed regulatory approval, except where the 12-week rule under SUP 10C applies to an emergency appointment, in which case the CCO will notify the relevant regulator on the first business day of the appointment.

### 7.4 Fitness and Propriety Assessments

In accordance with FIT 1 and FIT 2, Crest Bank will assess fitness and propriety against three criteria: honesty, integrity and reputation; competence and capability; and financial soundness.

- **At appointment:** People & Culture will conduct a full assessment prior to the individual commencing the role, including criminal record checks, credit checks, regulatory reference review, and verification of qualifications.
- **Annual reassessment:** People & Culture will complete a reassessment of all SMF holders and Certified Persons by 31 March each year, covering the preceding calendar year. Line managers will provide a written assessment of competence and capability as part of this process.
- **Triggered reassessment:** Where the CCO, CRO, or a line manager becomes aware of information that may affect an individual's fitness and propriety (including a conduct rule breach, a regulatory investigation, or a material change in financial circumstances), People & Culture will initiate an immediate reassessment. The CCO will determine within 5 business days whether regulatory notification is required.

### 7.5 Certification Regime

The CCO will maintain a register of all individuals performing Certification Functions, as defined under FCA rules. Crest Bank will issue a certificate to each Certified Person only where the annual fitness and propriety assessment confirms they meet the FIT criteria. Certificates are valid for 12 months. Where a certificate is not renewed, or is withdrawn, the individual must immediately cease performing the relevant Certification Function. People & Culture will notify the CCO on the same business day that a certificate is withheld or revoked. The CCO will assess whether FCA notification is required under SUP 10C.

### 7.6 Conduct Rules — Communication and Breach Reporting

The CCO will ensure that all staff receive written notification of the Conduct Rules applicable to them, in accordance with COCON. This notification will be provided at induction and whenever the rules are materially updated. Where a Conduct Rule breach by a Senior Manager is identified, the CCO will notify the FCA within 7 business days of the breach being confirmed. Conduct Rule breaches by other staff will be reported to the FCA annually in the prescribed return. All confirmed breaches will be recorded in the Conduct Rules Breach Register maintained by the CCO.

### 7.7 Regulatory References

When appointing a Senior Manager or Certified Person, People & Culture will request a regulatory reference from all relevant previous employers covering the preceding 6 years, in accordance with SYSC 22. When providing a reference, the CCO will ensure it is accurate, complete, and provided within 6 weeks of the request. Crest Bank will update a provided reference if material information comes to light after it has been issued.

## 8. Monitoring, Reporting and Escalation

| Activity | Frequency | Owner | Reported To |
|---|---|---|---|
| Management Responsibilities Map review | Quarterly (and within 7 days of any change) | CCO | Board Risk Committee |
| Fitness and propriety assessment completion | Annually (by 31 March) | People & Culture | CCO; Executive Risk Committee |
| Certification register review | Annually and on change | CCO | Compliance Oversight Committee |
| Conduct Rules Breach Register review | Monthly | CCO | Compliance Oversight Committee |
| Annual SM&CR compliance report | Annually | CCO | Board Risk Committee |
| Regulatory notification log | Ongoing; reviewed monthly | CCO | Executive Risk Committee |

**Escalation path:** Operational SM&CR issues are escalated by People & Culture or line managers to the CCO. Material concerns — including potential regulatory breaches, fitness and propriety failures, or gaps in the Management Responsibilities Map — are escalated by the CCO to the CRO and, where board-level awareness is required, to the Chair of the Board Risk Committee. The CCO retains responsibility for all regulatory notifications to the FCA and PRA.

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy will be reviewed annually, with the next scheduled review due by 31 January 2026. The CCO is responsible for initiating the review. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- A material change to FCA or PRA SM&CR rules or guidance;
- A regulatory finding or enforcement action relating to SM&CR at Crest Bank or, where directly relevant, at a comparable firm;
- A significant change to Crest Bank's organisational structure or senior management population;
- A material SM&CR breach identified through internal monitoring.

Amendments to this policy require approval by the Board Risk Committee. Minor corrections (typographical or factual updates that do not alter policy intent) may be approved by the CCO and noted at the next Board Risk Committee meeting.
