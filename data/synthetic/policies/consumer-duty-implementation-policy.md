## 2. Purpose

This policy sets out how Crest Bank Ltd meets its obligations under the FCA's Consumer Duty, as established by PS22/9 and codified in PRIN 2A of the FCA Handbook. It defines the standards Crest Bank applies to deliver good outcomes for retail customers across all products, services, and customer journeys. The policy provides a framework for embedding the Duty into governance, product design, communications, and customer support so that acting in the best interests of consumers is a consistent, measurable standard rather than a compliance exercise.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Business units:** Product, Customer Operations, Marketing, Technology, Credit Risk, Compliance, and all other functions that design, distribute, or service retail products.
- **Products and services:** All personal current accounts, instant-access savings accounts, fixed-term savings accounts, and personal loans offered to retail customers. SME business current accounts are excluded from Consumer Duty obligations but are subject to Crest Bank's separate Treating Customers Fairly framework.
- **Customer segments:** All retail customers, including those in vulnerable circumstances, throughout the full customer lifecycle (acquisition, onboarding, servicing, and closure).
- **Third parties:** Any distributor, introducer, or outsourced service provider acting in the distribution chain for Crest Bank retail products. Crest Bank, as manufacturer, retains responsibility for ensuring distributors meet the standards required by PRIN 2A.
- **Systems:** The Crest Bank mobile application, online banking platform, customer communications infrastructure, and any automated decision-making tools used in customer journeys.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to the following principles in implementing the Consumer Duty:

1. **Consumer Principle.** Crest Bank will act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers in accordance with PRIN 2A.1. This standard applies to all decisions, processes, and communications that affect retail customers, and takes precedence over narrower commercial considerations where the two conflict.

2. **Four Outcomes.** Crest Bank will meet its obligations under each of the four Consumer Duty outcome rules: products and services are fit for purpose and designed for an identified target market; prices are fair and represent genuine value; communications enable informed decision-making; and customer support is accessible, effective, and responsive.

3. **Fair Value.** Crest Bank will conduct and document fair value assessments for all in-scope retail products on at least an annual basis, and whenever a material change to a product's features, pricing, or target market is proposed.

4. **Vulnerable Customers.** Crest Bank will identify and respond appropriately to customers in vulnerable circumstances at every stage of the customer journey, ensuring that vulnerability does not result in worse outcomes.

5. **Governance and Accountability.** Consumer Duty obligations will be embedded in Crest Bank's governance framework. The Board Risk Committee will receive an annual Consumer Duty outcomes report. Senior managers with responsibility for in-scope activities will be accountable under the Senior Managers and Certification Regime (SM&CR) for compliance with this policy.

6. **Outcomes Monitoring.** Crest Bank will maintain a suite of quantitative and qualitative metrics to monitor whether good outcomes are being delivered across the four outcome areas. Where monitoring identifies evidence of poor outcomes, Crest Bank will act promptly to investigate root causes and implement remediation.

7. **Distribution Chain.** Where Crest Bank acts as manufacturer, it will share relevant information with distributors to enable them to comply with their own Consumer Duty obligations, and will take reasonable steps to satisfy itself that distributors are meeting the required standards, in accordance with PRIN 2A.

8. **Continuous Improvement.** Crest Bank will treat Consumer Duty as an ongoing standard, not a one-time implementation project. Lessons from outcomes monitoring, complaints analysis, and regulatory feedback will be used to improve products, services, and customer journeys on a continuous basis.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Products and Services Outcome

7.1.1 Before launching any new retail product or materially amending an existing one, the relevant product team will complete a **Consumer Duty Product Assessment**, documenting: the intended target market; the product's features and how they meet the target market's needs; foreseeable harms and mitigating controls; and distribution strategy.

7.1.2 The Product Governance Committee will review and approve the Consumer Duty Product Assessment prior to launch, in accordance with PRIN 2A and the product governance requirements of SYSC 4.1.

7.1.3 Crest Bank will maintain a **Target Market Statement** for each retail product, reviewed annually or following any material change. The CCO will maintain a register of all Target Market Statements.

7.1.4 Where Crest Bank distributes products through third parties, the CCO will ensure that distribution agreements include contractual obligations on distributors to comply with their Consumer Duty obligations and to share data necessary for Crest Bank's outcomes monitoring.

### 7.2 Price and Value Outcome

7.2.1 The CCO, working with the CRO and the relevant product owners, will conduct a **Fair Value Assessment** for each in-scope retail product at least annually. The assessment will document: the price paid by customers; the benefits received; costs to Crest Bank of providing the product; any differential outcomes between customer segments; and a reasoned conclusion on whether the product represents fair value.

7.2.2 Fair Value Assessments will be reviewed and approved by the Product Governance Committee. Where an assessment concludes that a product does not represent fair value, the product owner will submit a remediation plan to the Product Governance Committee within 30 calendar days.

7.2.3 In accordance with PRIN 2A, Crest Bank will not structure pricing in a way that exploits behavioural biases or creates loyalty penalties that result in worse outcomes for longstanding customers compared with new customers.

7.2.4 The CCO will maintain a **Fair Value Assessment Register**, recording the date of each assessment, the conclusion, and any remediation actions taken.

### 7.3 Consumer Understanding Outcome

7.3.1 All customer-facing communications — including in-app notifications, marketing materials, product terms, and support content — will be reviewed by the Compliance team against the consumer understanding standards in PRIN 2A and COBS 2.1 before publication.

7.3.2 Crest Bank will apply a **Plain Language Standard** to all retail customer communications. The CCO will maintain a Plain Language Checklist, which must be completed and signed off for all new or materially revised customer-facing documents.

7.3.3 Crest Bank will use customer testing, including usability research and comprehension testing, to validate that key communications (including product summaries, fee disclosures, and terms and conditions) are understood by the intended audience, including customers with lower financial capability. Results will be documented and retained for a minimum of five years.

7.3.4 Tom Hargreaves will ensure that the mobile app and online platform present information in a logical, accessible sequence that supports informed decision-making at key customer journey stages (e.g., product selection, loan application, account closure).

7.3.5 Where Crest Bank identifies through complaints data, customer research, or outcomes monitoring that a communication is causing confusion or leading to poor decisions, the relevant communication will be reviewed and revised within 60 calendar days of the identification.

### 7.4 Consumer Support Outcome

7.4.1 Priya Sharma will ensure that Crest Bank's customer support channels (in-app messaging, telephone, and email) are accessible to all retail customers, including those with accessibility needs, and that response times meet the service standards set out in the Customer Operations Service Standard.

7.4.2 Crest Bank will maintain a **Vulnerable Customer Policy** (see Related Documents) that sets out how front-line staff identify and respond to customers in vulnerable circumstances. All customer-facing staff will be trained on this policy in accordance with Section 9 of this document.

7.4.3 Crest Bank will not design processes that create unnecessary barriers to customers wishing to switch, close accounts, make complaints, or access support. Any process that requires a customer to take more steps to exit a product than to enter it will be escalated to the CCO for review.

7.4.4 Complaint handling will comply with DISP and will be monitored as part of the outcomes monitoring framework described in Section 8.

## 8. Monitoring, Reporting and Escalation

### 8.1 Outcomes Monitoring Framework

The CCO will maintain a **Consumer Duty Outcomes Monitoring Framework**, comprising the following minimum metrics reviewed on a monthly basis by the Compliance Oversight Committee:

| Outcome Area | Example Metrics |
|---|---|
| Products and Services | Product usage rates by target market segment; rates of product features used contrary to intended purpose |
| Price and Value | Net interest margin by product; fee income as a proportion of customer benefit; comparison with market benchmarks |
| Consumer Understanding | Comprehension test scores; rates of customers proceeding without reading key documents; complaint volumes attributable to misunderstanding |
| Consumer Support | Average resolution time; first-contact resolution rate; accessibility complaint volumes; vulnerable customer identification rate |

### 8.2 Reporting

- **Monthly:** The CCO presents a Consumer Duty dashboard to the Compliance Oversight Committee, highlighting any metrics outside tolerance and any emerging risks.
- **Monthly:** The CRO includes Consumer Duty risk in the Executive Risk Committee pack, with escalation of any material concerns to the Board Risk Committee.
- **Annually:** The CCO prepares a **Consumer Duty Annual Outcomes Report** for the Board Risk Committee. This report will assess whether Crest Bank is delivering good outcomes across all four outcome areas, document the results of fair value assessments, and set out any planned improvements. The Board Risk Committee will formally consider and minute its review of this report.

### 8.3 Escalation

- Where monthly monitoring identifies a metric outside agreed tolerance, the relevant business owner will submit a root cause analysis and remediation plan to the CCO within 10 business days.
- Where the CCO identifies evidence of systemic poor outcomes, the CCO will escalate to the CRO and, if material, to the Board Risk Committee within five business days.
- Where Crest Bank identifies that it has caused or is likely to cause material harm to retail customers, the CCO will assess whether the matter requires notification to the FCA under PRIN 2A and SYSC 6.1, and will advise the CEO accordingly.

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy is reviewed annually, with the next scheduled review due on **31 July 2024**. The CCO is responsible for initiating the review.

An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- Material FCA guidance, supervisory statement, or rule change relating to Consumer Duty or PRIN 2A.
- A finding from the Consumer Duty Annual Outcomes Report indicating that the policy framework is inadequate.
- A significant change to Crest Bank's product range, distribution model, or customer base.
- A regulatory investigation, enforcement action, or skilled person review relating to Consumer Duty.
- A recommendation from the Board Risk Committee or Executive Risk Committee.

Material amendments to this policy require approval by the Board Risk Committee. Minor amendments (e.g., updates to named individuals, correction of cross-references) may be approved by the CCO and ratified at the next Board Risk Committee meeting.
