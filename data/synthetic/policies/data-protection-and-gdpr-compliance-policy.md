## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's framework for processing personal data in compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018). It sets out the principles, obligations, and controls that govern how Crest Bank collects, uses, stores, shares, and deletes personal data relating to its customers, prospective customers, employees, and other data subjects. Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all staff and is essential to maintaining customer trust and Crest Bank's regulatory standing.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Staff:** All Crest Bank employees, contractors, consultants, and third parties acting on Crest Bank's behalf who process personal data in the course of their work.
- **Customer segments:** All retail personal current account and savings customers, personal loan customers, and SME business current account customers (including beneficial owners and authorised users of business accounts).
- **Products and systems:** All Crest Bank products and services, including the mobile application, online banking platform, customer onboarding systems, loan origination systems, marketing platforms, and internal HR systems.
- **Processing activities:** Any operation performed on personal data, whether automated or manual, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, transfer, and deletion.

This policy does not supersede Crest Bank's separate Employee Privacy Notice or Supplier Data Processing Agreements, but should be read alongside them.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to processing personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently. The following principles govern all personal data processing activities:

1. **Lawfulness, fairness and transparency:** Crest Bank will only process personal data where a valid lawful basis under UK GDPR Article 6 exists, and will provide clear, accessible privacy information to data subjects at the point of collection.
2. **Purpose limitation:** Personal data collected for a specified purpose will not be used for any incompatible secondary purpose without a fresh lawful basis or data subject consent.
3. **Data minimisation:** Crest Bank will collect and retain only the personal data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the stated processing purpose.
4. **Accuracy:** Crest Bank will take reasonable steps to ensure personal data is accurate and kept up to date, and will correct or delete inaccurate data promptly upon identification.
5. **Storage limitation:** Personal data will not be retained for longer than is necessary, in accordance with Crest Bank's Data Retention Schedule.
6. **Integrity and confidentiality:** Crest Bank will implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction, or damage, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 32.
7. **Accountability:** Crest Bank, as data controller, will demonstrate compliance with these principles and maintain records of processing activities (RoPA) as required by UK GDPR Article 30.
8. **Data protection by design and default:** Privacy considerations will be embedded into the design of new products, services, and systems from inception, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 25.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Establishing and Recording Lawful Bases for Processing

7.1.1 Before any new processing activity commences, the relevant business owner must document the intended lawful basis under UK GDPR Article 6 (and, where applicable, Article 9 for special category data or DPA 2018 Schedule 1 for criminal convictions data) in the RoPA.

7.1.2 The primary lawful bases applicable to Crest Bank's processing activities are:
- **Contract (Article 6(1)(b)):** Processing necessary to provide current accounts, savings products, and loans to customers.
- **Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)):** Processing required by AML, tax reporting, and regulatory obligations.
- **Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)):** Processing for fraud prevention, security monitoring, and certain direct marketing activities, subject to a documented Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA).
- **Consent (Article 6(1)(a)):** Processing for optional marketing communications, where no other basis applies.

7.1.3 The DPO will review and approve the lawful basis recorded for any new processing activity before it commences.

### 7.2 Privacy Notices and Transparency

7.2.1 Crest Bank will maintain a Customer Privacy Notice and an Employee Privacy Notice, each reviewed annually by the DPO and approved by the CCO.

7.2.2 The Customer Privacy Notice will be presented to all new customers at onboarding and will be accessible at all times via the Crest Bank mobile application and website. Any material changes to the notice will be communicated to existing customers with a minimum of 30 days' notice.

### 7.3 Data Subject Rights

7.3.1 Crest Bank will facilitate the exercise of all data subject rights under UK GDPR Articles 15–22, including the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.

7.3.2 Requests received through any channel (in-app, email, or written) will be logged by the Customer Operations team within one business day of receipt and assigned to a trained Data Rights Handler.

7.3.3 Crest Bank will respond to all valid data subject access requests (DSARs) within one calendar month of receipt. Where a request is complex or numerous, this period may be extended by a further two months, with the data subject notified of the extension and reasons within the initial one-month period, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 12.

7.3.4 Where Crest Bank refuses a request, the DPO will review the refusal before it is communicated to the data subject, and the data subject will be informed of their right to complain to the ICO.

### 7.4 Data Retention and Deletion

7.4.1 The DPO will maintain a Data Retention Schedule specifying the retention period for each category of personal data, based on legal obligation, regulatory requirement, or documented legitimate business need.

7.4.2 As a general principle, customer account data will be retained for six years following account closure, in line with the Limitation Act 1980 and FCA record-keeping requirements under SYSC 9.1. Data retained solely for AML purposes will be retained for five years from the end of the business relationship, in accordance with the Money Laundering Regulations 2017.

7.4.3 The CTO will ensure that automated deletion or anonymisation processes are implemented for data that has reached the end of its retention period. The DPO will audit compliance with the Retention Schedule annually.

### 7.5 Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)

7.5.1 In accordance with UK GDPR Article 35, a DPIA is mandatory before commencing any processing that is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals. Triggers include, but are not limited to: large-scale processing of special category data, systematic profiling, use of new technologies, and automated decision-making with significant effects.

7.5.2 The relevant business owner will initiate a DPIA request via the DPO at the earliest stage of project design. The DPO will complete or oversee the DPIA in consultation with the CTO and relevant business stakeholders.

7.5.3 Where a DPIA identifies a high residual risk that cannot be mitigated, the DPO will consult the ICO prior to processing commencing, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 36.

7.5.4 Completed DPIAs will be reviewed by the Product Governance Committee before sign-off for any new product or material system change.

### 7.6 Personal Data Breach Notification

7.6.1 Any staff member who discovers or suspects a personal data breach must report it to the DPO immediately, and no later than within four hours of discovery, using the Data Breach Reporting Form on Crest Bank's internal portal.

7.6.2 The DPO will assess the breach and, where it is not unlikely to result in a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms, will notify the ICO within 72 hours of Crest Bank becoming aware, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 33. The notification will include the information specified in Article 33(3).

7.6.3 Where a breach is likely to result in a high risk to affected individuals, the DPO will also notify those individuals without undue delay, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 34, unless an exemption applies.

7.6.4 All breaches, including those assessed as not requiring ICO notification, will be recorded in the Data Breach Register maintained by the DPO.

### 7.7 International Data Transfers

7.7.1 Crest Bank will not transfer personal data to a country outside the UK unless one of the following safeguards is in place, in accordance with UK GDPR Articles 44–49:
- The destination country benefits from a UK adequacy regulation.
- The transfer is governed by the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or UK Addendum to EU Standard Contractual Clauses.
- A specific derogation under Article 49 applies and is documented.

7.7.2 The DPO will maintain a register of all international transfers, including the safeguard relied upon for each. The CTO will ensure that no new third-party system or supplier involving international transfers is onboarded without prior DPO approval.

### 7.8 Data Protection by Design and Default

7.8.1 In accordance with UK GDPR Article 25 and SYSC 6.1, the CTO will ensure that privacy requirements are included in the technical specifications for all new systems and material changes to existing systems.

7.8.2 The Product Governance Committee will not approve a new product for launch unless a satisfactory DPIA has been completed and data minimisation principles have been applied to the product design.

## 9. Training

- **All staff** must complete Crest Bank's mandatory Data Protection Awareness training upon joining and annually thereafter. Completion is tracked via Crest Bank's Learning Management System (LMS).
- **Data Rights Handlers and Customer Operations staff** must complete an enhanced Data Subject Rights training module before handling DSARs, and refresher training annually.
- **Product managers, engineers, and data analysts** must complete Privacy by Design training before working on any project involving personal data processing.
- **Senior management and Board members** must receive an annual data protection briefing prepared by the DPO, covering regulatory developments and Crest Bank's compliance performance.
- The DPO will report training completion rates to the Compliance Oversight Committee quarterly. Any team with a completion rate below 95% will be subject to a targeted remediation plan agreed with the relevant Head of Department.

---

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy will be reviewed annually by the DPO, with approval by the Board Risk Committee. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- A material change to UK GDPR, DPA 2018, or ICO guidance.
- An ICO investigation, enforcement notice, or fine involving Crest Bank.
- A significant personal data breach or near-miss that reveals a gap in this policy.
- A material change to Crest Bank's processing activities, products, or technology infrastructure.
- A recommendation from the Compliance Oversight Committee or Board Risk Committee.

Proposed amendments must be reviewed by the DPO and CCO before submission to the Board Risk Committee for approval.
