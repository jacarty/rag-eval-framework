## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank Ltd's framework for the handling, segregation, and safeguarding of client money and assets in accordance with the FCA's Client Assets Sourcebook (CASS). It sets out the obligations of relevant staff to ensure that client money is protected at all times and can be promptly returned to clients in the event of Crest Bank's insolvency or operational failure. The policy also governs the maintenance of the CASS resolution pack and the ongoing reconciliation and record-keeping obligations that underpin Crest Bank's CASS compliance.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Business lines and products:** Personal current accounts, instant-access and fixed-term savings accounts, personal loans, and SME business current accounts, to the extent that funds held in those products constitute client money under CASS 7.
- **Teams and functions:** Finance, Customer Operations, Technology, Compliance, and Risk.
- **Systems:** Core banking platform, reconciliation systems, and any third-party payment or custody systems through which client money flows or is held.
- **Customer segments:** All retail customers (approximately 850,000) and SME customers (approximately 12,000).
- **Third parties:** Any bank, credit institution, or third-party payment service provider with which Crest Bank places or holds client money.

This policy does not apply to Crest Bank's own proprietary funds, which are governed separately under the Treasury Policy.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank adopts the following principles in relation to client money and assets:

1. **Segregation:** Crest Bank will at all times hold client money separately from its own funds, in accordance with CASS 7.13. Client money will be placed in one or more designated client bank accounts, clearly identified as such, with approved credit institutions.

2. **Acknowledgement letters:** Before placing client money with a third-party bank or credit institution, Crest Bank will obtain a written acknowledgement from that institution confirming that the money is held on behalf of clients and is not available to satisfy the institution's claims against Crest Bank, in accordance with CASS 7.18.

3. **Reconciliation:** Crest Bank will perform daily internal reconciliation and periodic external reconciliation of client money balances to ensure that the amount held in client bank accounts matches Crest Bank's records of client entitlements, in accordance with CASS 7.15 and CASS 7.16.

4. **Record keeping:** Crest Bank will maintain accurate, complete, and up-to-date records of all client money held, received, and disbursed, sufficient to enable prompt identification and return of each client's entitlement at any time.

5. **CASS resolution pack:** Crest Bank will maintain and regularly test a CASS resolution pack in accordance with CASS 10, ensuring it contains all information necessary to facilitate the rapid return of client money in the event of Crest Bank's insolvency or failure.

6. **Diversification:** Crest Bank will apply appropriate diversification when selecting and placing client money with third-party institutions, taking into account credit quality, concentration risk, and the need to protect client interests.

7. **Oversight and governance:** The CCO, supported by the CASS Oversight function within Compliance, will maintain day-to-day oversight of CASS compliance. Material breaches or shortfalls will be escalated promptly to the Executive Risk Committee and, where required, reported to the FCA.

8. **Training and competence:** All staff with responsibilities under this policy will receive role-appropriate CASS training and will demonstrate ongoing competence in their CASS obligations.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Client Money Segregation

1. Upon receipt of any funds that constitute client money under CASS 7, the Finance Team will ensure those funds are credited to a designated client bank account no later than the business day following receipt.
2. Designated client bank accounts must be held with a credit institution approved by the CCO and CRO, following assessment of the institution's credit quality and regulatory standing.
3. In accordance with CASS 7.13, no Crest Bank proprietary funds will be commingled with client money in a designated client bank account, except where a small buffer amount is required to maintain the account and has been approved by the CCO.
4. The Finance Team will maintain a register of all designated client bank accounts, including account names, account numbers, holding institutions, and the date each acknowledgement letter was obtained.

### 7.2 Acknowledgement Letters

1. Before placing client money with any third-party institution, the Compliance Team will obtain a written acknowledgement letter from that institution in the form required by CASS 7.18.
2. Acknowledgement letters will be reviewed annually by the Compliance Team to confirm they remain current and that the holding institution's status has not changed materially.
3. Where a holding institution declines to provide or renew an acknowledgement letter, the CCO will be notified immediately and Crest Bank will cease placing new client money with that institution pending resolution.

### 7.3 Reconciliation

1. **Internal reconciliation:** The Finance Team (CASS Reconciliation Lead) will perform a daily internal reconciliation comparing the aggregate balance held in all designated client bank accounts against the sum of individual client entitlements recorded in the core banking system, in accordance with CASS 7.15.
2. **External reconciliation:** The Finance Team will perform an external reconciliation at least monthly, comparing Crest Bank's internal records against statements or confirmations received from each holding institution, in accordance with CASS 7.16.
3. Any discrepancy identified during internal or external reconciliation must be investigated and resolved within one business day of identification. Where a shortfall is identified, the Finance Team will immediately notify the CCO and top up the relevant client bank account from Crest Bank's own funds pending investigation.
4. Reconciliation records, including the methodology, inputs, outputs, and any discrepancies identified and resolved, will be retained for a minimum of five years.

### 7.4 Record Keeping

1. Crest Bank will maintain records sufficient to identify, at any point in time, the total amount of client money held and each individual client's entitlement, in accordance with CASS 7.
2. Records will be stored in the core banking system and backed up daily to a geographically separate location managed by the Technology team under the oversight of Tom Hargreaves.
3. Client money records will be retained for a minimum of five years from the date of the relevant transaction or the closure of the client relationship, whichever is later, consistent with SUP 16 and Crest Bank's Records Management Policy.
4. Access to client money records will be restricted to authorised personnel on a need-to-know basis, in accordance with Crest Bank's Information Security Policy.

### 7.5 CASS Resolution Pack

1. In accordance with CASS 10, Crest Bank will maintain a CASS resolution pack containing, at minimum: a list of all designated client bank accounts; copies of all acknowledgement letters; a description of the systems and processes used to hold and administer client money; the most recent reconciliation records; and contact details for key personnel and third-party institutions.
2. The CASS resolution pack will be maintained by the Compliance Team and reviewed and updated at least quarterly, or immediately following any material change to Crest Bank's client money arrangements.
3. The CCO will approve the CASS resolution pack at each quarterly review and will confirm its adequacy to the Board Risk Committee annually.
4. Crest Bank will conduct an annual test of the resolution pack to verify that an insolvency practitioner or administrator could use it to identify and return client money within the timeframes required by CASS 10. The results of each test will be documented and any remediation actions tracked to completion.
5. The resolution pack will be stored in a secure, readily accessible location, with access granted to the CCO, CRO, CEO, and any appointed insolvency practitioner. Tom Hargreaves will ensure that the pack remains accessible in the event of a system outage.

### 7.6 Diversification of Client Money Placements

1. The CRO and CCO will jointly review the concentration of client money held with any single institution at least quarterly.
2. No more than 30% of total client money under management will be placed with any single third-party institution without the prior approval of the Executive Risk Committee.
3. Where a holding institution's credit rating falls below investment grade, the Finance Team will notify the CCO and CRO immediately, and Crest Bank will initiate a transfer of client money to an alternative approved institution within five business days.

## 9. Training

- All staff in Finance, Customer Operations, and Compliance with CASS responsibilities must complete mandatory CASS foundation training upon joining Crest Bank and annually thereafter.
- The CASS Reconciliation Lead and Compliance Team (CASS Oversight) must complete role-specific advanced CASS training, covering reconciliation methodology and resolution pack maintenance, within three months of appointment and annually thereafter.
- Training completion is recorded in Crest Bank's Learning Management System (LMS). The CCO will review completion rates quarterly and report any gaps to the Compliance Oversight Committee.
- Where regulatory changes materially affect CASS obligations, the Compliance Team will design and deploy targeted refresher training within 60 days of the change taking effect.

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy will be reviewed annually by the CCO and approved by the Board Risk Committee. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- A material change to CASS rules or FCA guidance affecting Crest Bank's obligations.
- A significant CASS breach or client money shortfall.
- A material change to Crest Bank's products, systems, or third-party arrangements affecting client money.
- A recommendation arising from the annual resolution pack test or an internal audit finding.
- Direction from the FCA or PRA.

Proposed amendments to this policy must be reviewed by the CCO and CRO before submission to the Board Risk Committee for approval.
