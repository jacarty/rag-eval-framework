## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's framework for identifying, controlling, and managing inside information and for preventing market abuse in connection with Crest Bank's operations, its customers, and its staff. It sets out the obligations of all relevant persons under the UK Market Abuse Regulation (UK MAR) and the FCA's market conduct rules, and ensures that Crest Bank maintains robust systems and controls proportionate to the nature of its business. Compliance with this policy is essential to protect market integrity, preserve customer trust, and safeguard Crest Bank's regulatory standing.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **All Crest Bank employees**, including permanent staff, contractors, secondees, and senior managers, regardless of seniority or business function.
- **All business lines**, including personal current accounts, savings products, personal loans, and SME business current accounts.
- **All systems and channels** through which Crest Bank staff access, process, or transmit customer or counterparty information, including internal platforms, third-party integrations, and communication tools.
- **Third parties** acting on Crest Bank's behalf (e.g. outsourced service providers, introducers) where contractual arrangements require compliance with equivalent standards.

This policy is of particular relevance to staff in Finance, Treasury, Credit Risk, Product, Legal, Compliance, and Customer Operations, and to any employee who may receive or handle information relating to publicly listed companies through their customer-facing or operational duties.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to upholding market integrity and to ensuring that inside information is identified, controlled, and protected at all times. The following principles govern Crest Bank's approach:

1. **Zero tolerance for market abuse.** Crest Bank will not engage in, facilitate, or ignore conduct that constitutes insider dealing, unlawful disclosure of inside information, or market manipulation, whether by its staff, customers, or counterparties.

2. **Prompt identification of inside information.** Any information that meets the definition of inside information under UK MAR must be identified as such at the earliest practicable opportunity and subjected to the controls set out in this policy.

3. **Strict information barriers.** Inside information must be contained within defined groups of authorised individuals. Crest Bank will maintain information barriers (including logical access controls and physical segregation where appropriate) to prevent the unauthorised flow of inside information across business lines.

4. **Mandatory insider lists.** Wherever inside information arises in connection with Crest Bank's activities, a formal insider list must be created and maintained in accordance with UK MAR Article 18 requirements.

5. **Controlled wall-crossing.** Any deliberate disclosure of inside information to a person not already on the relevant insider list (a "wall-crossing") must follow Crest Bank's prescribed procedure and must be documented before disclosure is made.

6. **Personal account dealing restrictions.** Staff who possess, or who may possess, inside information are subject to restrictions on personal account dealing in the relevant financial instruments, as set out in Section 7.4 of this policy.

7. **Mandatory suspicious transaction and order reporting.** Where Crest Bank, in the course of its operations, identifies a transaction or order that could constitute market abuse, it will submit a Suspicious Transaction and Order Report (STOR) to the FCA in accordance with UK MAR Article 16 and SUP 15.3.

8. **Culture of escalation.** All staff are expected to escalate concerns about potential market abuse or misuse of inside information promptly and without fear of retaliation. Crest Bank will not penalise any employee who raises a concern in good faith.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Identification of Inside Information

7.1.1 A member of staff who believes they have received or generated information that may constitute inside information under UK MAR must immediately notify the Compliance team by raising a Market Conduct Query via Crest Bank's internal compliance portal.

7.1.2 The Compliance team will assess whether the information meets the four-limb test under UK MAR Article 7: (i) precise; (ii) not publicly available; (iii) relating directly or indirectly to one or more issuers or financial instruments; and (iv) if made public, likely to have a significant effect on the price of the relevant financial instruments.

7.1.3 Where the Compliance team determines that inside information exists, it will notify the CCO within two business hours and initiate the insider list procedure (Section 7.2) without delay.

7.1.4 Pending a determination, the staff member must treat the information as inside information and must not trade, disclose, or act upon it.

### 7.2 Insider Lists

7.2.1 In accordance with UK MAR Article 18, the Compliance team will create and maintain a separate insider list for each discrete piece of inside information identified at Crest Bank.

7.2.2 Each insider list will record, at minimum: the full name and job title of each insider; the reason for inclusion; the date and time the individual obtained access to the inside information; and the date and time of any removal from the list.

7.2.3 Before being added to an insider list, each individual must receive written notification of their inclusion, the nature of their obligations under UK MAR, and the consequences of breach. The Compliance team will obtain written acknowledgement from each individual.

7.2.4 Insider lists will be maintained in Crest Bank's secure compliance document management system. Access is restricted to the Compliance team and the CCO. Lists will be retained for a minimum of five years from the date of creation, in accordance with UK MAR Article 18(5).

7.2.5 The CCO will review all active insider lists at least fortnightly and will close lists promptly once the relevant information ceases to be inside information (e.g. upon public disclosure).

### 7.3 Wall-Crossing Procedures

7.3.1 A "wall-crossing" occurs when inside information is intentionally disclosed to a person who is not already on the relevant insider list. No wall-crossing may take place without prior written approval from the CCO.

7.3.2 The requesting party must submit a Wall-Crossing Request Form via the compliance portal, setting out: the identity of the proposed recipient; the business rationale for disclosure; the specific inside information to be disclosed; and confirmation that the disclosure is necessary and proportionate.

7.3.3 Upon approval, the Compliance team will add the recipient to the relevant insider list and provide them with the required notification and acknowledgement documentation before any disclosure is made.

7.3.4 Where a wall-crossing involves a third party external to Crest Bank, the CCO must be satisfied that equivalent confidentiality controls are in place at the recipient organisation before approving the request.

7.3.5 All wall-crossing approvals and rejections will be logged in the compliance portal with a timestamp and the CCO's rationale. In accordance with SYSC 10.1, this record forms part of Crest Bank's conflicts of interest documentation.

### 7.4 Personal Account Dealing Restrictions

7.4.1 Any employee who is placed on an insider list is immediately subject to a dealing restriction in the relevant financial instruments. The Compliance team will notify the individual in writing at the point of inclusion on the list.

7.4.2 Restricted individuals must not: execute or instruct any trade (directly or through a nominee) in the relevant financial instruments; cancel or amend existing orders in those instruments; or recommend or encourage another person to trade in those instruments.

7.4.3 All employees (whether or not currently on an insider list) must obtain pre-clearance from the Compliance team before executing any personal account deal in a financial instrument that could be connected to Crest Bank's business activities or customer relationships. Pre-clearance requests must be submitted via the compliance portal and will be determined within one business day.

7.4.4 Pre-clearance will be refused where the Compliance team has reasonable grounds to believe the employee may possess inside information, or where the proposed trade could give rise to a conflict of interest under SYSC 10.1.

7.4.5 Employees must report all personal account deals in financial instruments to the Compliance team within one business day of execution, regardless of whether pre-clearance was required. The Compliance team will maintain a personal account dealing register.

### 7.5 Suspicious Transaction and Order Reporting (STORs)

7.5.1 In accordance with UK MAR Article 16, Crest Bank is required to report to the FCA any transaction or order that it reasonably suspects may constitute market abuse.

7.5.2 Any employee who identifies a customer transaction, order, or pattern of behaviour that may constitute market abuse must escalate this to the Compliance team immediately, and in any event within the same business day, using the internal suspicious activity referral process on the compliance portal.

7.5.3 The Compliance team will assess the referral against the indicators of market abuse set out in the FCA's MAR 1 guidance and relevant ESMA guidelines on STORs (as retained and adapted for UK purposes). The assessment will be completed within three business days of receipt.

7.5.4 Where the Compliance team determines that a STOR is required, the CCO will submit the report to the FCA via the FCA's STOR submission portal. Submission will occur as soon as practicable and, in any event, no later than the end of the business day following the determination. In accordance with SUP 15.3, Crest Bank will not delay submission pending the outcome of any internal investigation.

7.5.5 The CCO will notify the MLRO of all STOR submissions to enable assessment of whether a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) to the National Crime Agency is also required.

7.5.6 All STOR referrals, assessments, and submissions will be recorded in the compliance portal. Records will be retained for a minimum of five years.

## 8. Monitoring, Reporting and Escalation

8.1 The Compliance team will conduct monthly reviews of: all active insider lists; personal account dealing pre-clearance requests and refusals; STOR referrals and submissions; and wall-crossing requests.

8.2 The CTO will provide the Compliance team with monthly reports from Crest Bank's communication surveillance and access log systems, enabling the Compliance team to identify potential breaches of information barriers.

8.3 The CCO will present a quarterly Market Conduct Report to the Board Risk Committee, covering: the number and status of insider lists; wall-crossing activity; personal account dealing statistics; STOR activity; and any identified or suspected breaches of this policy.

8.4 The CCO will provide a standing update to the Compliance Oversight Committee at each monthly meeting, covering material developments since the previous report.

8.5 **Escalation path:** Individual employee concern → Compliance team → CCO → Executive Risk Committee (if systemic or material) → Board Risk Committee. Where a matter requires immediate FCA notification under SUP 15.3, the CCO will escalate directly to the CEO and notify the FCA without waiting for committee approval.

8.6 Any confirmed or suspected breach of this policy will be logged as a compliance incident and managed in accordance with Crest Bank's Incident Management Policy. Material breaches will be reported to the Board Risk Committee at the next available meeting and, where required, to the FCA.

## 9. Training

9.1 All employees must complete Crest Bank's mandatory Market Abuse Awareness training module upon joining and annually thereafter. Completion is tracked via Crest Bank's learning management system (LMS).

9.2 Employees in Finance, Treasury, Credit Risk, Legal, Compliance, and Customer Operations must additionally complete an enhanced Market Conduct training module, covering insider list obligations, wall-crossing procedures, and STOR requirements. This module must be completed within 30 days of joining and annually thereafter.

9.3 Any employee placed on an insider list for the first time must receive a targeted briefing from the Compliance team before being added to the list, covering their specific obligations and the consequences of breach.

9.4 The CCO will review training completion rates quarterly. Any employee with an overdue training module will be escalated to their line manager and, if not remediated within 10 business days, to the Executive Risk Committee.

9.5 Training content will be reviewed annually by the Compliance team and updated to reflect changes in regulation, FCA guidance, or lessons learned from internal incidents or industry developments.

---

## 10. Review Cycle

10.1 This policy will be reviewed annually by the CCO, with the updated version submitted to the Board Risk Committee for approval.

10.2 An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- Material changes to UK MAR or related FCA Handbook provisions;
- FCA supervisory feedback or enforcement action relevant to market conduct;
- A material breach of this policy or a significant STOR submission;
- A significant change to Crest Bank's business model, product range, or customer base that creates new market conduct risk;
- Industry-wide guidance or Dear CEO letters from the FCA addressing market abuse controls.

10.3 Minor amendments (e.g. updates to named individuals, process clarifications that do not alter policy intent) may be approved by the CCO without full Board Risk Committee sign-off, provided they are reported to the Board Risk Committee at the next quarterly meeting.
