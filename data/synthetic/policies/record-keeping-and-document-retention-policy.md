## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's obligations and standards for the creation, maintenance, storage, retrieval, and disposal of records across all business functions. It ensures that Crest Bank meets its regulatory record-keeping obligations and can demonstrate compliance to the FCA, PRA, and other competent authorities upon request. It also protects Crest Bank's legal interests and supports operational continuity by ensuring that records are accurate, accessible, and retained for appropriate periods.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Staff:** All Crest Bank employees, contractors, and third-party service providers acting on Crest Bank's behalf.
- **Products and customer segments:** All personal current accounts, instant-access and fixed-term savings products, personal loans, and SME business current accounts, covering both retail (approximately 850,000 customers) and SME (approximately 12,000 customers) segments.
- **Systems and formats:** All records held in any format — electronic, structured database, cloud-hosted, or otherwise — including records held within Crest Bank's core banking platform, CRM systems, document management systems, email and messaging platforms, and any third-party systems used to process or store Crest Bank data.
- **Functions:** Operations, Compliance, Risk, Finance, Technology, Legal, HR, and Customer Operations.

This policy does not govern the handling of personal data beyond record-keeping obligations; data protection requirements are addressed separately in the Data Protection and Privacy Policy.

## 4. Regulatory Context

The following FCA Handbook modules and legislative instruments are the primary sources of Crest Bank's record-keeping obligations:

| Source | Relevance |
|---|---|
| **SYSC 9.1** (General record-keeping requirements) | Requires firms to keep orderly records of their business and internal organisation sufficient to enable the FCA to monitor compliance with regulatory requirements. |
| **SYSC 6.1** (Compliance function) | Requires firms to maintain records that demonstrate the effectiveness of compliance monitoring and the firm's adherence to regulatory obligations. |
| **COBS 11.8** (Record-keeping for client orders and transactions) | Requires firms to retain records of all orders received and transactions executed for a minimum period. |
| **COBS 2.1** (Client communications and fair treatment) | Supports record-keeping of customer communications to evidence fair treatment and suitability. |
| **BCOBS 5** (Banking: communications with banking customers) | Requires firms to retain records of communications with retail banking customers. |
| **PRIN 2** (Skill, care and diligence) | Underpins the obligation to maintain accurate and complete records as part of conducting business with due skill, care and diligence. |
| **The Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (MLRs 2017), Regulation 40** | Requires retention of customer due diligence records, transaction records, and supporting documentation for a minimum of five years following the end of a business relationship or transaction. |
| **UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018** | Governs the lawful basis for retaining personal data and requires that data is not kept longer than necessary for its stated purpose. |
| **Companies Act 2006** | Requires retention of certain corporate and accounting records for prescribed statutory periods. |

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank commits to the following principles in respect of record keeping and document retention:

1. **Completeness and accuracy:** Crest Bank will create and maintain records that are complete, accurate, and sufficient to demonstrate compliance with all applicable regulatory, legal, and contractual obligations.
2. **Defined retention periods:** All records will be retained for the minimum periods specified in the Retention Schedule at Section 7.2, and will not be retained beyond those periods unless a legal hold or other legitimate business reason applies.
3. **Secure and accessible storage:** Records will be stored in systems and formats that protect their integrity, prevent unauthorised access or alteration, and enable timely retrieval upon request by regulators, auditors, or authorised internal staff.
4. **Lawful disposal:** Records will be disposed of securely and in accordance with the procedures set out in Section 7.4 once their retention period has expired and no legal hold is in force.
5. **Legal hold compliance:** Where litigation, regulatory investigation, or a formal regulatory request is anticipated or in progress, Crest Bank will suspend routine disposal and preserve all relevant records until the hold is formally lifted.
6. **Regulatory accessibility:** Records must be capable of being produced to the FCA, PRA, or other competent authority promptly and in a readable format, in accordance with SYSC 9.1.
7. **Staff accountability:** All staff are personally responsible for creating accurate records of their activities and for handling records in accordance with this policy.
8. **Audit trail integrity:** Crest Bank will maintain tamper-evident audit trails for all material transactions, customer interactions, and compliance-relevant decisions.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Record Creation

All staff must create a contemporaneous record of any material decision, customer interaction, transaction, or compliance-relevant activity. Records must be created in Crest Bank's designated systems and must not be held solely in personal email accounts, local device storage, or unofficial messaging channels. In accordance with SYSC 9.1, records must be sufficient to enable reconstruction of the relevant activity and to demonstrate regulatory compliance.

### 7.2 Retention Schedule

The following minimum retention periods apply. Where a legal hold is in force, records must be retained until the hold is lifted regardless of the periods below.

| Record Category | Examples | Minimum Retention Period | Regulatory Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Customer due diligence (CDD) and KYC records | Identity documents, verification records, PEP/sanctions screening | 5 years from end of business relationship | MLRs 2017, Reg. 40 |
| Transaction records | Payment instructions, transfers, loan drawdowns | 5 years from date of transaction | MLRs 2017, Reg. 40; COBS 11.8 |
| Customer account records | Account opening documentation, account statements | 6 years from account closure | BCOBS 5; Limitation Act 1980 |
| Customer communications | Emails, in-app messages, call recordings, complaints correspondence | 3 years (retail); 5 years (investment-related communications) | COBS 2.1; BCOBS 5 |
| Complaints records | Complaint logs, investigation notes, FOS referrals, outcomes | 3 years from date of complaint resolution | DISP 1.9 |
| Loan and credit records | Loan agreements, credit assessments, repayment records | 6 years from end of agreement | Limitation Act 1980; CONC |
| AML Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) | Internal SARs, NCA disclosures, tipping-off records | 5 years from date of report | MLRs 2017, Reg. 40 |
| Compliance monitoring records | Monitoring plans, testing results, breach logs | 5 years | SYSC 6.1 |
| Board and committee minutes | Board Risk Committee, Executive Risk Committee minutes | 10 years | Companies Act 2006 |
| HR and employment records | Contracts, disciplinary records, training completion | 6 years from end of employment | Employment law; SYSC 5.1 |
| Financial and accounting records | Management accounts, audited accounts, ledgers | 6 years | Companies Act 2006 |
| IT and system audit logs | Access logs, change management records, security event logs | 3 years | SYSC 9.1; SYSC 13 |
| Regulatory correspondence | FCA/PRA letters, supervisory notices, responses | 10 years | SYSC 9.1 |

### 7.3 Storage Requirements

Records must be stored in accordance with the following standards:

- **Designated systems only:** All records must be held within Crest Bank-approved systems. The CTO will maintain and publish a current list of approved storage systems.
- **Access controls:** Access to records must be restricted on a need-to-know basis. The CTO will ensure role-based access controls are applied and reviewed annually.
- **Integrity and immutability:** Records must be stored in a format that prevents unauthorised alteration. Where records are stored electronically, write-once or audit-logged storage must be used for records subject to regulatory retention requirements, in accordance with SYSC 9.1.
- **Backup and resilience:** All records subject to regulatory retention must be backed up in accordance with Crest Bank's Business Continuity and IT Resilience Policy, with backups stored in a geographically separate location.
- **Legibility and retrievability:** Records must remain legible and retrievable throughout their retention period. The CTO will conduct annual checks to identify and remediate any records at risk of format obsolescence.

### 7.4 Disposal Procedures

Upon expiry of the applicable retention period, and provided no legal hold is in force, records must be disposed of as follows:

1. The relevant system owner or line manager will identify records approaching or past their retention period during the annual records review (see Section 8).
2. The system owner will confirm with the DPO that disposal is consistent with UK GDPR obligations and with the CCO that no legal hold applies.
3. Electronic records will be deleted using a method that renders them unrecoverable, in accordance with Crest Bank's Data Disposal Standard (a sub-document maintained by the CTO).
4. Any physical records (e.g. original identity documents held temporarily) will be destroyed by cross-cut shredding or by a contracted secure destruction service. A certificate of destruction must be obtained and retained for 3 years.
5. Disposal must be logged in the Records Disposal Register, recording the record category, date of disposal, method, and the names of the individuals who authorised and confirmed disposal.

### 7.5 Legal Hold Process

1. **Trigger:** A legal hold may be triggered by receipt of a regulatory information request, commencement of litigation, a formal investigation, or a reasonable anticipation of any of the above. The CCO, CRO, or MLRO may initiate a legal hold.
2. **Notification:** The CCO will issue a Legal Hold Notice to all relevant staff and system owners, identifying the scope of records to be preserved and the reason for the hold. The CTO will implement a technical suspension of automated deletion for the identified record sets.
3. **Suspension of disposal:** All routine disposal activity for records within the scope of the hold must cease immediately upon receipt of the Legal Hold Notice.
4. **Register:** The CCO will maintain a Legal Hold Register recording the date of each hold, its scope, the triggering event, and the date of lifting.
5. **Lifting the hold:** The CCO will formally lift the hold in writing once the relevant proceedings, investigation, or regulatory request has concluded. Normal retention and disposal procedures will resume from the date of lifting.

## 8. Monitoring, Reporting and Escalation

- **Annual records review:** The CCO will coordinate an annual review of all record categories to verify that records are being created, stored, and disposed of in accordance with this policy. Findings will be reported to the Compliance Oversight Committee.
- **System audit log review:** The CTO will provide a quarterly report to the Compliance Oversight Committee on system access logs, storage integrity checks, and any incidents affecting record availability or integrity.
- **Retention compliance monitoring:** The CCO will conduct quarterly spot-checks on a sample of record categories to verify compliance with the Retention Schedule.
- **Escalation path:** Suspected material breaches of this policy (including unauthorised deletion, suspected tampering, or failure to comply with a legal hold) must be escalated immediately to the CCO. The CCO will escalate to the Board Risk Committee if the breach is material or if it may constitute a regulatory breach requiring notification to the FCA or PRA.
- **Regulatory reporting:** Where a record-keeping failure may constitute a breach of FCA or PRA requirements, the CCO will assess whether notification is required under SUP 15.3 and will manage any required disclosure.

## 9. Training

- **Mandatory induction training:** All new staff must complete record-keeping and document retention training as part of their induction programme, prior to handling any customer or regulatory records.
- **Annual refresher training:** All staff must complete an annual refresher module covering this policy, any changes to the Retention Schedule, and legal hold obligations. Completion is mandatory and must be achieved within 30 days of the module being assigned.
- **Role-specific training:** Staff in Compliance, Risk, Legal, and the MLRO function must complete additional training on regulatory record-keeping obligations (including MLRs 2017 requirements) on an annual basis.
- **Tracking:** Training completion is tracked via Crest Bank's learning management system (LMS). The CCO receives a monthly completion report. Non-completion is escalated to the relevant line manager after 14 days and to the CCO after 30 days.

This policy will be reviewed annually, with the next scheduled review due by 1 February 2026. The CCO is responsible for initiating and coordinating the review.

- A recommendation from the Board Risk Committee or an internal or external audit.
