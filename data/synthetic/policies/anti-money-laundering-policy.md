## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's framework for detecting, preventing, and reporting money laundering and terrorist financing activity in accordance with applicable UK law and regulatory requirements. It sets out the obligations of all staff in identifying and managing financial crime risk across Crest Bank's customer base and product range. Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all relevant persons and forms a core component of Crest Bank's financial crime control environment.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Staff:** All Crest Bank employees, contractors, and third-party agents who interact with customers, process transactions, or have access to customer account data.
- **Products:** Personal current accounts, instant-access savings, fixed-term savings, personal loans, and SME business current accounts.
- **Customer segments:** All retail and SME customers, prospective customers undergoing onboarding, and any third parties acting on behalf of customers.
- **Systems:** The core banking platform, transaction monitoring system, KYC/onboarding platform, and any third-party data providers used for identity verification or screening.

This policy does not apply to Crest Bank's employment or procurement functions except where those functions involve customer-facing financial activity.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to maintaining a robust and proportionate anti-money laundering framework. The following principles govern Crest Bank's approach:

1. **Risk-based approach.** Crest Bank will assess and document the money laundering and terrorist financing risks associated with its customer base, products, delivery channels, and geographies, and will apply controls proportionate to those risks, in accordance with Regulation 18 of the MLRs 2017.

2. **Customer due diligence.** Crest Bank will not establish or maintain a business relationship with any customer unless satisfactory CDD has been completed, or a permitted exception under the MLRs 2017 applies and is documented.

3. **Enhanced due diligence.** Crest Bank will apply EDD measures to all higher-risk relationships, including politically exposed persons (PEPs), customers from high-risk third countries, and any relationship presenting indicators of elevated financial crime risk.

4. **Ongoing monitoring.** Crest Bank will continuously monitor existing customer relationships to ensure that transactions are consistent with Crest Bank's knowledge of the customer and their risk profile, and that CDD information remains current and accurate.

5. **Suspicious activity reporting.** All staff have a legal obligation to report knowledge or suspicion of money laundering or terrorist financing to the MLRO. Crest Bank will not engage in tipping off, and will submit SARs to the National Crime Agency (NCA) where required.

6. **Record-keeping.** Crest Bank will retain CDD records, transaction records, and SAR-related documentation for a minimum of five years from the end of the business relationship or the date of the transaction, in accordance with Regulation 40 of the MLRs 2017.

7. **Training and awareness.** Crest Bank will ensure that all relevant staff receive adequate AML training commensurate with their role and exposure to financial crime risk.

8. **No facilitation.** Crest Bank will not knowingly facilitate money laundering or terrorist financing, and will take prompt action to exit relationships where such activity is confirmed or strongly suspected.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

In accordance with Regulation 28 of the MLRs 2017, Crest Bank will apply standard CDD measures to all customers at onboarding. CDD comprises:

1. **Identity verification (retail customers):** Collecting full legal name, date of birth, and residential address. Verifying identity electronically using Crest Bank's KYC platform against authoritative data sources. Where electronic verification is inconclusive, requesting documentary evidence (e.g. passport, driving licence, utility bill).

2. **Identity verification (SME customers):** Verifying the legal entity name, registered number, and registered address via Companies House. Identifying and verifying the identity of all beneficial owners holding 25% or more of shares or voting rights, and all individuals exercising significant control, in accordance with Regulation 28(3) of the MLRs 2017.

3. **Purpose and nature of relationship:** Collecting information on the intended purpose of the account and the expected nature and volume of transactions at onboarding.

4. **Risk scoring:** Assigning each customer an initial AML risk score (Low, Medium, or High) based on customer type, product, geography, and transaction profile. Risk scores are recorded in the core banking platform and reviewed at defined intervals.

CDD must be completed before a business relationship is established. Where CDD cannot be completed, the onboarding process will be terminated and, where suspicion exists, an internal SAR will be raised.

### 7.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

In accordance with Regulation 33 of the MLRs 2017, EDD measures are mandatory for:

- Customers identified as **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)** or their close associates and family members;
- Customers or transactions with a nexus to **high-risk third countries** as designated by HM Treasury;
- Any customer assigned a **High** AML risk score at onboarding or during periodic review;
- Any relationship where the MLRO or a senior compliance officer has determined that higher risk is present.

EDD measures will include, as appropriate:

1. Obtaining senior management approval (at Head of Customer Operations level or above) before establishing or continuing the relationship.
2. Collecting additional information on the source of funds and source of wealth, with documentary corroboration where reasonably obtainable.
3. Conducting enhanced ongoing monitoring, including more frequent transaction review and lower alert thresholds in the transaction monitoring system.
4. Documenting the rationale for accepting the relationship and the specific EDD measures applied.

### 7.3 PEP Screening

1. All customers are screened against PEP databases at onboarding using Crest Bank's automated screening tool.
2. Screening is repeated on a rolling basis and triggered by name-change events or other profile updates.
3. Positive matches generate an alert to the Compliance team for manual review within two business days.
4. Confirmed PEPs are escalated to the MLRO, who will determine whether the relationship may proceed subject to EDD, or whether it should be declined.
5. Crest Bank will treat domestic PEPs and foreign PEPs proportionately, applying a risk-based assessment in line with JMLSG Guidance Part I, Chapter 4.

### 7.4 Ongoing Monitoring

In accordance with Regulation 28(11) of the MLRs 2017 and SYSC 6.1, Crest Bank will:

1. Operate an automated transaction monitoring system configured with rules and thresholds calibrated to Crest Bank's customer risk profile and product set. The CTO is responsible for the technical maintenance of this system.
2. Review and update transaction monitoring rules at least annually, or following a material change in Crest Bank's product range or customer base.
3. Conduct periodic CDD refresh reviews: Low-risk customers every three years; Medium-risk customers every two years; High-risk and EDD customers annually, or more frequently if triggered by a material change or alert.
4. Investigate all transaction monitoring alerts within five business days of generation. Unresolved alerts will be escalated to the MLRO if not resolved within ten business days.

### 7.5 Suspicious Activity Reporting (SARs)

1. Any member of staff who knows or suspects that a person is engaged in money laundering or terrorist financing must submit an internal SAR to the MLRO promptly, and in any event before taking any further action on the relevant account or transaction. Internal SARs are submitted via Crest Bank's designated secure reporting channel.
2. Staff must not disclose to the customer or any third party that a SAR has been filed or is under consideration (tipping-off prohibition under section 333A of POCA 2002).
3. The MLRO will review each internal SAR and determine within seven calendar days whether to submit an external SAR to the NCA via the Suspicious Activity Reports Online system.
4. Where a Defence Against Money Laundering (DAML) is required, the MLRO will seek consent from the NCA before proceeding with the relevant transaction or activity.
5. The MLRO will maintain a SAR register recording all internal reports received, decisions made, and external SARs submitted. This register is confidential and accessible only to the MLRO, CCO, and, where legally required, regulatory authorities.

### 7.6 Record-Keeping

In accordance with Regulation 40 of the MLRs 2017, Crest Bank will retain:

- CDD documents and verification records for five years from the end of the business relationship;
- Transaction records sufficient to reconstruct individual transactions for five years from the date of the transaction;
- SAR-related records (internal reports, MLRO decisions, NCA correspondence) for five years from the date of the report.

Records will be stored securely and made available to the FCA, NCA, or other competent authorities upon lawful request.

|---|---|---|---|

| Independent AML effectiveness review | Annually | Internal Audit | Board Risk Committee |

**Escalation path:** Operational alerts → Compliance Operations → MLRO → CCO → Executive Risk Committee → Board Risk Committee. Material issues (e.g. systemic monitoring failures, regulatory investigations) are escalated directly to the Board Risk Committee by the MLRO or CCO without delay.

## 9. Training

- **All staff** must complete Crest Bank's mandatory AML awareness training at induction and annually thereafter. This training covers the legal framework, recognition of suspicious activity, and the internal SAR process.
- **Customer Operations and Compliance staff** must complete role-specific AML training covering CDD, EDD, and transaction monitoring procedures, refreshed annually.
- **The MLRO and Compliance team** must maintain up-to-date knowledge of AML developments, including NCA typologies, FCA thematic reviews, and JMLSG guidance updates, evidenced by CPD records.
- Training completion is tracked via Crest Bank's learning management system. Non-completion is reported to line managers and escalated to the CCO if not remediated within 30 days of the deadline.
- The MLRO will report training completion rates to the Compliance Oversight Committee quarterly.

---

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy is reviewed annually by the MLRO, with approval by the Board Risk Committee. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- A material change in Crest Bank's products, customer segments, or delivery channels;
- An amendment to the MLRs 2017, POCA 2002, TACT 2000, or relevant FCA rules;
- A significant AML incident, regulatory finding, or internal audit recommendation;
- Publication of material new JMLSG guidance or FCA thematic review findings relevant to Crest Bank's business model.

Proposed amendments are drafted by the MLRO, reviewed by the CCO, and submitted to the Board Risk Committee for approval before taking effect.
