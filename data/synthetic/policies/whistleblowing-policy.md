## 2. Purpose

This policy sets out Crest Bank's commitment to maintaining an open and accountable culture in which all workers feel safe to raise concerns about wrongdoing, regulatory breaches, or other malpractice without fear of retaliation. It establishes the mechanisms through which concerns may be reported, the protections afforded to those who raise them, and the obligations placed on Crest Bank to investigate and, where required, report matters to the FCA and PRA. The policy implements Crest Bank's obligations under the FCA's whistleblowing rules and the Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998 (PIDA).

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **People:** All Crest Bank employees (permanent, fixed-term, and part-time), contractors, agency workers, consultants, and former employees raising concerns about conduct during their engagement. It also extends to workers supplied through third-party arrangements where Crest Bank is the principal employer.
- **Products and Business Lines:** All Crest Bank products and services, including personal current accounts, savings products, personal loans, and SME business current accounts.
- **Systems and Channels:** All internal and external reporting channels described in Section 7 of this policy.
- **Geographic Scope:** All Crest Bank operations conducted from its UK headquarters and any remote-working arrangements.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank adopts the following core principles in relation to whistleblowing:

1. **Openness:** Crest Bank is committed to fostering a culture in which concerns about wrongdoing are welcomed, taken seriously, and acted upon promptly.
2. **Protection from retaliation:** No worker who makes a qualifying disclosure in good faith will be subjected to dismissal, demotion, harassment, disciplinary action, or any other detriment as a result of raising a concern. Any act of retaliation against a whistleblower constitutes a serious disciplinary matter.
3. **Confidentiality:** The identity of a worker raising a concern will be protected to the fullest extent practicable. Where disclosure of identity is necessary for a fair investigation, Crest Bank will notify the worker in advance wherever possible.
4. **Anonymous reporting:** Crest Bank accepts anonymous reports and will investigate them to the extent that the information provided permits, recognising that anonymity may limit the scope of any investigation.
5. **Independence:** Concerns may be raised outside normal management lines. Workers are never required to report to their direct line manager and may escalate directly to the Whistleblowing Champion, the CCO, or external bodies.
6. **Regulatory transparency:** Crest Bank will inform all workers of their right to report concerns directly to the FCA or PRA at any time, and will not discourage or obstruct any worker from exercising that right, in accordance with SYSC 18.5.
7. **Accountability:** All disclosures will be recorded, investigated proportionately, and closed with documented outcomes. The Whistleblowing Champion will report on the operation of these arrangements to the Board Risk Committee.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Reporting Channels

Workers may raise a concern through any of the following channels:

1. **Internal Whistleblowing Hotline:** A dedicated telephone line and secure online portal, operated independently of line management and accessible 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Contact details are published on Crest Bank's internal intranet and in the employee handbook.
2. **Direct report to the CCO:** Workers may contact Rachel Whitfield directly by email or in person. The CCO's contact details are published on the intranet.
3. **Direct report to the Whistleblowing Champion:** Workers may contact the Whistleblowing Champion directly, particularly where the concern relates to the conduct of senior management or where previous reports have not been addressed.
4. **External reporting to the FCA:** In accordance with SYSC 18.5, workers may report concerns directly to the FCA at any time using the FCA's whistleblowing service (email: whistle@fca.org.uk; telephone: 020 7066 9200). Crest Bank will not discourage, penalise, or obstruct any worker who chooses this route.
5. **External reporting to the PRA:** Workers may also report concerns to the PRA via its dedicated whistleblowing channel.
6. **Prescribed persons under PIDA:** Workers may report to other prescribed persons (e.g. the Prudential Regulation Authority, the Financial Ombudsman Service, or HMRC) where the concern falls within that body's remit.

Workers are encouraged, but not required, to use internal channels before reporting externally.

### 7.2 Appointment of the Whistleblowing Champion

In accordance with SYSC 18.4, Crest Bank appoints a Senior Manager to the role of Whistleblowing Champion. The current Whistleblowing Champion is **Rachel Whitfield, CCO**, holding Senior Manager Function responsibility for this role. The appointment is recorded in Crest Bank's Responsibilities Map submitted to the FCA. If the Whistleblowing Champion role becomes vacant, the CEO (Sarah Chen) will assume interim responsibility and a permanent appointment will be made within 30 days.

### 7.3 Receiving and Logging Disclosures

1. All disclosures received through any channel will be logged in Crest Bank's secure Whistleblowing Case Register within one business day of receipt.
2. The Case Register records: date of receipt, channel used, nature of the concern, identity of the discloser (or "anonymous" where applicable), assigned investigator, and current status.
3. Access to the Case Register is restricted to the CCO, Whistleblowing Champion, MLRO (for financial crime matters), and the DPO (for data protection oversight). Line managers do not have access.
4. The CCO will acknowledge receipt of a disclosure to the discloser (where identity is known) within three business days.

### 7.4 Initial Assessment

1. Within five business days of logging, the CCO will conduct an initial assessment to determine: (a) whether the disclosure constitutes a qualifying disclosure under PIDA; (b) whether it raises a potential regulatory breach requiring notification to the FCA or PRA; (c) whether it has an AML or financial crime dimension requiring MLRO involvement; and (d) the appropriate investigation track (internal investigation, HR process, or referral to an external body).
2. Where the concern implicates a member of the Executive Committee or the Whistleblowing Champion themselves, the CCO will refer the matter directly to Michael Byrne (Chair, Board Risk Committee) to appoint an independent investigator.

### 7.5 Investigation

1. Investigations will be conducted by a suitably qualified and independent investigator, appointed by the CCO, who has no material conflict of interest in the matter.
2. The investigator will prepare a written investigation plan within ten business days of appointment, setting out the scope, methodology, and estimated timeline.
3. Investigations will be conducted confidentially. The identity of the discloser will not be shared with the subject of the investigation without the discloser's prior consent, except where legally required.
4. The investigator will produce a written findings report, including recommended remedial actions, upon conclusion of the investigation.
5. The CCO will communicate the outcome to the discloser (where identity is known) in writing, subject to any legal constraints on disclosure.

### 7.6 Regulatory Notification

1. In accordance with SYSC 18.5, where an investigation concludes that a reportable concern exists — including a potential breach of FCA or PRA rules, market abuse, or a matter affecting the fitness and propriety of a Senior Manager — the CCO will notify the FCA and/or PRA without undue delay.
2. The CCO will maintain a log of all regulatory notifications made under this section, which will be reviewed by the Board Risk Committee.

### 7.7 Protection Against Retaliation

1. Any worker who believes they have suffered a detriment as a result of making a disclosure must report this to the CCO or Whistleblowing Champion immediately.
2. The CCO will initiate a separate investigation into the alleged retaliation within five business days of the complaint being received.
3. Where retaliation is substantiated, Crest Bank will take disciplinary action against the responsible individual(s), up to and including dismissal.
4. Workers retain the right to bring an employment tribunal claim under PIDA independently of Crest Bank's internal process.

## 9. Training

1. **All staff** must complete mandatory whistleblowing awareness training upon joining Crest Bank and annually thereafter. This training covers: the types of concern that may be raised, available reporting channels, protections under PIDA, and Crest Bank's non-retaliation commitment.
2. **Line managers** must complete an enhanced module covering their specific obligations, including how to handle a concern raised with them and the prohibition on discouraging disclosures.
3. **Investigators** appointed under Section 7.5 must have completed investigation skills training approved by the CCO before conducting a whistleblowing investigation.
4. Training completion is recorded in Crest Bank's Learning Management System (LMS). The People & Culture Team provides monthly completion reports to the CCO. Non-completion is escalated to the relevant line manager within ten business days of the deadline.

---

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy is reviewed annually by the CCO, with approval by the Board Risk Committee. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- A material change to SYSC 18 or related FCA/PRA guidance.
- An amendment to PIDA or other relevant legislation.
- A significant whistleblowing case that reveals a gap in Crest Bank's arrangements.
- A regulatory finding or recommendation relating to Crest Bank's whistleblowing framework.
- A material change to Crest Bank's business model, structure, or product range.

All amendments to this policy require approval by the Board Risk Committee before taking effect. Minor typographical or formatting corrections may be approved by the CCO and noted at the next Board Risk Committee meeting.
