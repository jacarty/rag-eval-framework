## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's framework for identifying, managing and strengthening its operational resilience, ensuring that Crest Bank can prevent, adapt to, respond to, recover from and learn from operational disruptions. It sets out how Crest Bank identifies its important business services, sets impact tolerances, and tests its ability to remain within those tolerances. The policy implements the requirements of the PRA Rulebook and FCA Handbook as they relate to operational resilience, and supports Crest Bank's overarching obligation to deliver good outcomes for its retail and SME customers.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **All Crest Bank staff**, including permanent employees, contractors and secondees, across all business functions.
- **All important business services** as identified and maintained in the Important Business Services Register (see Section 7.1).
- **All technology systems and infrastructure** that support the delivery of important business services, including cloud-hosted platforms, core banking systems, payment rails and mobile/web channels.
- **All third-party and outsourced service providers** whose services underpin important business services, including critical technology vendors, payment processors and cloud infrastructure providers.
- **All customer segments**: personal current account holders, savings customers, personal loan customers and SME business current account holders.

This policy does not supersede the Business Continuity Policy or the IT Disaster Recovery Policy, but operates alongside them as the overarching resilience framework.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to the following principles in managing its operational resilience:

1. **Identification of important business services**: Crest Bank will maintain an up-to-date register of all important business services — those whose disruption could cause intolerable harm to customers, market integrity or financial stability — and will review this register at least annually and following any material change to Crest Bank's business model or operating environment.

2. **Setting impact tolerances**: For each important business service, Crest Bank will set a single impact tolerance that defines the maximum tolerable duration and level of disruption. Impact tolerances will be expressed in measurable terms and will be set with reference to the potential harm to customers and, where applicable, systemic impact.

3. **Mapping people, processes, technology and third parties**: Crest Bank will maintain documented maps of the resources — including people, processes, technology systems, data and third-party dependencies — that underpin each important business service, to a level of granularity sufficient to support effective scenario testing and recovery planning.

4. **Scenario testing**: Crest Bank will conduct regular, rigorous scenario testing to assess whether it can remain within its impact tolerances during severe but plausible disruptions. Testing will include third-party failure scenarios and technology outage scenarios relevant to Crest Bank's digital-only operating model.

5. **Third-party and outsourcing resilience**: Crest Bank will ensure that contractual arrangements with all critical third-party providers include resilience obligations, and will assess third-party resilience as part of both onboarding due diligence and ongoing oversight.

6. **Incident response and recovery**: Crest Bank will maintain documented response and recovery plans for each important business service, enabling rapid mobilisation in the event of a disruption and ensuring that customer communications are timely and accurate.

7. **Self-assessment and continuous improvement**: Crest Bank will maintain a written self-assessment of its operational resilience, updated at least annually, and will use lessons learned from incidents and testing to drive continuous improvement.

8. **Governance and accountability**: The Board Risk Committee retains ultimate oversight of operational resilience. The CRO holds day-to-day executive accountability, and all material resilience risks will be escalated through defined governance channels.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Identifying and Maintaining the Important Business Services Register

In accordance with SYSC 15A, Crest Bank will identify its important business services by assessing which services, if disrupted, could cause intolerable harm to customers or pose a risk to market integrity or financial stability. The initial identification exercise will consider:

- The nature and volume of customers relying on the service;
- The availability of alternative means for customers to access equivalent services;
- The potential for financial loss, distress or harm to customers;
- Any systemic significance of the service.

The CRO will maintain the Important Business Services Register, which will be reviewed and approved by the Board Risk Committee annually and following any material change to Crest Bank's products, systems or operating model. Current important business services include, but are not limited to: personal current account access (including payments and card services), savings account access and withdrawals, personal loan origination and servicing, and SME business current account access and payments.

### 7.2 Setting Impact Tolerances

For each important business service, the CRO, working with the relevant Business Service Owner and Priya Sharma, will set a single impact tolerance expressed as the maximum tolerable duration of disruption. Impact tolerances will be:

- Expressed in objective, measurable terms (e.g. maximum hours of service unavailability, maximum number of failed transactions);
- Set with primary reference to potential harm to customers, consistent with Crest Bank's obligations under PRIN 2A;
- Approved by the Board Risk Committee before coming into effect;
- Reviewed at least annually or following a material incident or change to the service.

### 7.3 Mapping Important Business Services

In accordance with PRA SS1/21, Crest Bank will produce and maintain documented maps for each important business service. Maps will identify:

- **People**: key roles and minimum staffing requirements;
- **Processes**: critical process steps and dependencies;
- **Technology**: systems, applications, data stores and infrastructure components;
- **Facilities**: relevant to Crest Bank's digital operating model, this includes data centres and cloud regions;
- **Third parties**: all outsourced providers and critical vendors whose failure could affect the service.

Maps will be reviewed annually by the relevant Business Service Owner and validated by the CTO for technology components. Material changes to mapped resources will trigger an immediate update.

### 7.4 Scenario Testing

In accordance with SYSC 15A, Crest Bank will conduct scenario testing at least annually to assess whether it can remain within its impact tolerances. The CRO will approve an annual scenario testing plan, which will include:

- At least one severe but plausible technology outage scenario (e.g. cloud provider failure or core banking system unavailability);
- At least one critical third-party failure scenario;
- At least one cyber incident scenario, coordinated with the CTO;
- At least one scenario involving simultaneous disruption to multiple important business services.

Testing will involve relevant Business Service Owners, the CTO, and where appropriate, critical third-party providers. Results will be documented, including identified gaps and remediation actions. The CRO will present scenario testing results to the Executive Risk Committee and, in summary, to the Board Risk Committee.

### 7.5 Third-Party Resilience Management

In accordance with SYSC 8.1, Crest Bank will manage third-party resilience risk as follows:

- **Onboarding**: all new critical third-party providers must complete a resilience due diligence assessment before contract execution, covering their own business continuity arrangements, recovery time objectives and incident notification procedures.
- **Contractual requirements**: contracts with critical third-party providers must include obligations to maintain resilience standards consistent with Crest Bank's impact tolerances, to notify Crest Bank of incidents within defined timeframes, and to participate in joint scenario testing where required.
- **Ongoing oversight**: the CTO will conduct annual resilience reviews of critical third-party providers, with results reported to the Executive Risk Committee. Material concerns will be escalated to the CRO.

### 7.6 Incident Response and Recovery

When a disruption to an important business service occurs or is anticipated:

1. The relevant Business Service Owner will notify the CRO and CTO within **one hour** of identifying a potential breach or imminent risk of breaching an impact tolerance.
2. The CRO will convene an Operational Resilience Incident Response Group, comprising the CTO, Priya Sharma, Rachel Whitfield and other relevant parties, within **two hours** of notification.
3. Priya Sharma will activate the customer communication plan for the affected service, ensuring customers are informed of the disruption and expected resolution timeframe via Crest Bank's digital channels.
4. The CTO will implement the relevant IT Disaster Recovery procedures and provide status updates to the Incident Response Group at intervals of no greater than **30 minutes** during an active incident.
5. Where an impact tolerance is breached or is at material risk of being breached, the CRO will notify the FCA and PRA in accordance with regulatory notification requirements and Crest Bank's Regulatory Reporting Policy.
6. Following resolution, the Business Service Owner will produce a post-incident review report within **ten business days**, covering root cause, timeline, customer impact and remediation actions.

### 7.7 Self-Assessment

The CCO, working with the CRO, will maintain Crest Bank's written operational resilience self-assessment document. The self-assessment will be updated at least annually and will document:

- Crest Bank's important business services and their impact tolerances;
- The outcomes of mapping and scenario testing exercises;
- Identified vulnerabilities and the remediation actions taken or planned;
- An assessment of Crest Bank's ability to remain within impact tolerances.

The self-assessment will be approved by the Board Risk Committee and made available to the PRA and FCA on request.

## 9. Training

- All staff with responsibilities under this policy (Business Service Owners, technology teams, customer operations staff) must complete operational resilience awareness training upon joining Crest Bank and annually thereafter.
- Staff with specific incident response roles must complete scenario-based resilience training, including tabletop exercises, at least annually.
- Training completion is recorded in Crest Bank's Learning Management System. The CCO will report completion rates to the Compliance Oversight Committee quarterly. Non-completion will be escalated to the relevant line manager and, where persistent, to the CRO.

---

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy is reviewed annually, with the next scheduled review due by **31 January 2026**. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- A material change to Crest Bank's important business services or operating model;
- A significant operational incident that results in a breach of an impact tolerance;
- Material changes to PRA or FCA operational resilience requirements or supervisory guidance;
- A recommendation from the Board Risk Committee or Executive Risk Committee.

All amendments to this policy require approval by the Board Risk Committee. Minor editorial corrections may be approved by the CRO and noted at the next Board Risk Committee meeting.
