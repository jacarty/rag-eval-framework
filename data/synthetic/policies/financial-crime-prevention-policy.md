## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's framework for identifying, preventing, detecting, and responding to financial crime across all business activities. It sets out the obligations of all staff and governance bodies in protecting Crest Bank, its customers, and the wider financial system from fraud, money laundering, terrorist financing, sanctions violations, and bribery and corruption. Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all Crest Bank personnel and supports Crest Bank's obligations as a dual-regulated firm under FCA Firm Reference Number 924173.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Personnel:** All Crest Bank employees, contractors, secondees, and members of the Board, regardless of seniority or function.
- **Products and services:** Personal current accounts, instant-access and fixed-term savings accounts, personal loans, and SME business current accounts.
- **Customer segments:** All retail customers (approximately 850,000) and SME customers (approximately 12,000).
- **Systems:** All technology platforms, payment rails, onboarding systems, transaction monitoring tools, and third-party integrations operated by or on behalf of Crest Bank.
- **Geographic reach:** All activities conducted from Crest Bank's UK operations, including any cross-border transactions processed on behalf of customers.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to the highest standards of financial crime prevention. The following principles govern Crest Bank's approach:

1. **Zero tolerance for financial crime facilitation.** Crest Bank will not knowingly facilitate, enable, or ignore any form of financial crime, including money laundering, fraud, terrorist financing, sanctions evasion, or bribery and corruption.

2. **Risk-based approach.** Crest Bank will assess and manage financial crime risk proportionately, applying enhanced controls where the risk of harm is greatest, in accordance with the risk-based approach required by the MLRs 2017 and FCG 1.

3. **Three lines of defence.** Crest Bank operates a three lines of defence model. The first line (business operations) owns and manages financial crime risk day-to-day. The second line (Compliance and Risk functions) provides oversight, policy, and challenge. The third line (Internal Audit) provides independent assurance over the effectiveness of controls.

4. **Customer due diligence.** Crest Bank will verify the identity of all customers at onboarding and apply ongoing monitoring proportionate to the risk they present, ensuring Crest Bank does not provide services to those who pose an unacceptable financial crime risk.

5. **Sanctions compliance.** Crest Bank will screen all customers, transactions, and counterparties against applicable UK sanctions lists in real time and will freeze assets and report to OFSI without delay where a match is confirmed.

6. **Fraud prevention.** Crest Bank will deploy layered controls to detect and prevent fraud, including authorised push payment (APP) fraud, account takeover, and first-party fraud, protecting both customers and Crest Bank from financial loss.

7. **Mandatory reporting.** All staff have a legal obligation to report knowledge or suspicion of money laundering or terrorist financing to the MLRO. Failure to report is a criminal offence under POCA 2002 and TACT 2000. Crest Bank will never penalise staff for making a report in good faith.

8. **Culture and accountability.** Crest Bank's Board and Executive Committee are accountable for embedding a culture in which financial crime prevention is treated as a shared responsibility, and in which staff feel empowered to raise concerns without fear of retaliation.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

In accordance with the MLRs 2017 and SYSC 6.3, Crest Bank applies CDD to all customers at onboarding and on a risk-triggered basis thereafter.

1. **Standard CDD** is applied to all new customers and requires: verification of identity using documentary and electronic means; verification of the nature and purpose of the business relationship; and ongoing monitoring of transactions.
2. **Simplified CDD** may be applied where a customer presents a demonstrably low risk, as documented in Crest Bank's Customer Risk Assessment methodology.
3. **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)** is mandatory for: Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their associates; customers from high-risk third countries as designated by the FATF; and any customer or transaction flagged as high-risk by Crest Bank's risk scoring model. EDD requires senior management approval, source of funds and source of wealth verification, and enhanced ongoing monitoring.
4. The Head of Customer Operations will ensure that no account is activated until CDD checks are satisfactorily completed.

### 7.2 Ongoing Transaction Monitoring

1. Crest Bank's automated transaction monitoring system screens all transactions in real time against behavioural baselines, typology rules, and peer-group comparisons.
2. Alerts generated by the system are reviewed by the first-line Financial Crime Operations team within the timeframes specified in the Transaction Monitoring Procedures document.
3. Alerts that cannot be resolved at first-line review are escalated to the MLRO within 24 hours.
4. The CTO will ensure the transaction monitoring system is subject to regular tuning reviews, at minimum quarterly, to maintain detection effectiveness and manage alert volumes.

### 7.3 Suspicious Activity Reporting

In accordance with POCA 2002 and TACT 2000:

1. Any member of staff who knows or suspects that a person is engaged in money laundering or terrorist financing must submit an internal SAR to the MLRO promptly using Crest Bank's internal reporting system. Staff must not tip off the subject of the report.
2. The MLRO will evaluate each internal SAR within five business days of receipt and will either: submit an external SAR to the NCA via the NCA's online portal; or document the reasons for not submitting with reference to the legal threshold.
3. Where a Defence Against Money Laundering (DAML) consent is required before proceeding with a transaction, the MLRO will apply for consent from the NCA and will instruct the relevant team to hold the transaction pending a response.
4. The MLRO will maintain a complete register of all internal and external SARs, which is available to the FCA and PRA upon request.

### 7.4 Sanctions Screening

In accordance with SAMLA 2018 and OFSI guidance:

1. All customers are screened against UK consolidated sanctions lists at onboarding. Screening is repeated on a daily batch basis and in real time at the point of each payment instruction.
2. Potential sanctions matches are reviewed by the Financial Crime Operations team within four business hours of the alert being generated.
3. Where a match is confirmed, the MLRO and CCO are notified immediately. The relevant account is frozen without delay, and a report is submitted to OFSI within the timeframe required by applicable sanctions regulations.
4. Crest Bank will not process any payment to or from a sanctioned person or entity, and will not unfreeze assets without written authorisation from OFSI.

### 7.5 Fraud Detection and Prevention

1. Crest Bank deploys layered fraud controls including device fingerprinting, behavioural biometrics, velocity checks, and real-time payment intervention for suspected APP fraud, consistent with the requirements of the PSR's APP Fraud Reimbursement Scheme.
2. The fraud detection system generates alerts that are triaged by the Customer Operations team. Accounts subject to a confirmed fraud event are suspended pending investigation.
3. Crest Bank will contact customers in real time where a payment is assessed as high-risk, providing a friction step and fraud warning in accordance with the Contingent Reimbursement Model Code and PSR requirements.
4. Confirmed fraud events are reported to Action Fraud and, where appropriate, to the NCA. Material fraud incidents are escalated to the CRO within 24 hours.

### 7.6 Bribery and Corruption Prevention

In accordance with the Bribery Act 2010:

1. Crest Bank prohibits the offering, giving, requesting, or receiving of any bribe, whether directly or through a third party.
2. All staff must declare gifts and hospitality above £50 in value using Crest Bank's Gifts and Hospitality Register, maintained by the Compliance function.
3. Crest Bank conducts proportionate due diligence on all third-party suppliers and introducers to assess bribery and corruption risk before engagement.
4. Any suspicion of bribery or corruption must be reported to the CCO immediately. The CCO will determine whether a referral to law enforcement is required.

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy is reviewed annually by the MLRO and CCO, with approval by the Board Risk Committee. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- A material change in applicable legislation, FCA/PRA rules, or OFSI/FATF guidance.
- A significant financial crime incident or near-miss that reveals a gap in the policy framework.
- A material change to Crest Bank's products, customer segments, or technology infrastructure.
- A finding by Internal Audit or an external reviewer that identifies a policy deficiency.
- A regulatory communication (including a Dear CEO letter) that requires a policy response.

All amendments require approval by the Board Risk Committee before taking effect. Minor typographical or formatting corrections may be approved by the CCO and noted at the next Board Risk Committee meeting.
