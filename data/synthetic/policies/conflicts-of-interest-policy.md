## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's framework for identifying, recording, managing, and disclosing conflicts of interest that arise in the course of its regulated activities. It ensures that Crest Bank acts in the best interests of its customers and maintains the integrity of its operations, in accordance with its obligations as a dual-regulated firm. Where conflicts cannot be adequately managed, this policy sets out the steps Crest Bank will take to prevent harm to customers and to the firm.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Staff:** All Crest Bank employees, contractors, secondees, and Senior Managers performing functions on behalf of Crest Bank, regardless of seniority or employment status.
- **Products and services:** All regulated and unregulated products and services offered by Crest Bank, including personal current accounts, savings products, personal loans, and SME business current accounts.
- **Customer segments:** Retail and SME customers.
- **Systems:** All platforms, tools, and processes used to deliver Crest Bank's products and services, including its core banking platform, CRM systems, and third-party integrations.

This policy does not apply to Crest Bank's parent entities or subsidiaries (if any) except where staff of those entities are performing functions on behalf of Crest Bank.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to the following principles in managing conflicts of interest:

1. **Identification:** Crest Bank will take all reasonable steps to identify conflicts of interest that arise or may arise in the course of carrying out regulated activities, including conflicts between Crest Bank and its customers, and between one customer and another.

2. **Recording:** All identified conflicts of interest — whether actual, potential, or perceived — will be recorded in Crest Bank's Conflicts of Interest Register. This register will be maintained, reviewed, and updated on an ongoing basis.

3. **Management:** Where a conflict is identified, Crest Bank will implement appropriate controls to manage it. These controls will be proportionate to the nature and risk of the conflict. Where a conflict cannot be adequately managed, Crest Bank will consider whether the relevant activity should be restricted or declined.

4. **Disclosure:** Where organisational or procedural controls are insufficient to ensure, with reasonable confidence, that risks of damage to customer interests will be prevented, Crest Bank will disclose the nature and source of the conflict to the affected customer before undertaking the relevant activity.

5. **Personal Conduct:** All staff are expected to avoid situations in which their personal interests conflict, or may appear to conflict, with their duties to Crest Bank or its customers. This includes obligations relating to personal account dealing, gifts and hospitality, and outside business interests.

6. **Independence of Judgement:** No member of staff will allow personal relationships, financial interests, or external commitments to influence their professional judgement or the advice and services provided to customers.

7. **Culture and Accountability:** Crest Bank's Board and Senior Management are responsible for fostering a culture in which conflicts are identified and escalated promptly, without fear of adverse consequence.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Identification of Conflicts

In accordance with SYSC 10.1.3R, Crest Bank will identify conflicts of interest by considering, at a minimum, whether Crest Bank or a relevant person:

- stands to make a financial gain, or avoid a financial loss, at the expense of a customer;
- has an interest in the outcome of a service or transaction that differs from the customer's interest;
- has a financial or other incentive to favour the interests of one customer over another;
- carries on the same business as a customer; or
- receives or will receive an inducement from a person other than the customer in relation to a service provided to that customer.

The Compliance team will conduct a conflicts identification exercise at least annually, covering all business lines, products, and material third-party relationships. New products and material changes to existing products must be assessed for conflicts of interest as part of the Product Governance Committee approval process.

### 7.2 Conflicts of Interest Register

The Compliance team will maintain a Conflicts of Interest Register, as required by SYSC 10.1.10R. The register will record, for each identified conflict:

- a description of the nature and source of the conflict;
- the parties affected (by role or customer segment, not by individual customer name);
- the controls or mitigants in place;
- the date of identification and the date of last review;
- the owner responsible for managing the conflict; and
- whether disclosure to customers has been made or is required.

The register will be reviewed and updated at least quarterly by the CCO. Any new conflict identified by a member of staff must be reported to the Compliance team within five business days of identification, using the Conflict Declaration Form available on Crest Bank's intranet.

### 7.3 Management of Conflicts

Upon identification of a conflict, the Compliance team will assess the appropriate management response. Controls may include, but are not limited to:

- **Information barriers:** Restricting the flow of information between teams where one team holds information that could be used to the detriment of a customer or another team.
- **Segregation of duties:** Ensuring that staff involved in a conflicted activity are not involved in the oversight or approval of that activity.
- **Recusal:** Requiring an individual to withdraw from a decision-making process where they have a personal interest in the outcome.
- **Independent review:** Requiring a decision or recommendation to be reviewed by a person with no conflict.
- **Restriction of activity:** Prohibiting Crest Bank from undertaking a particular activity where no adequate management control can be identified.

Where a conflict cannot be managed to an acceptable standard, the CCO will escalate the matter to the Executive Risk Committee for a decision on whether the relevant activity should proceed.

### 7.4 Disclosure of Conflicts

Where management controls are insufficient to prevent the risk of customer detriment with reasonable confidence, Crest Bank will disclose the conflict to the affected customer in accordance with SYSC 10.1.8G. Disclosure will:

- be made before Crest Bank undertakes the relevant activity;
- describe the nature and source of the conflict in plain, clear language;
- be provided in a durable medium; and
- not be used as a substitute for implementing adequate management controls.

Disclosure templates will be maintained by the Compliance team and reviewed annually. Priya Sharma will ensure that customer-facing staff are familiar with the disclosure process.

### 7.5 Personal Account Dealing

All staff must comply with Crest Bank's Personal Account Dealing Rules, which form a schedule to this policy. Key requirements include:

- Staff must not deal in financial instruments where they hold material non-public information, in accordance with MAR 1.
- Staff in designated roles (as defined in the Personal Account Dealing Rules) must pre-clear transactions above defined thresholds with the Compliance team before execution.
- All personal account transactions by designated staff must be reported to Compliance within two business days of execution.
- Compliance will maintain a log of pre-clearance requests and approvals, which will be reviewed quarterly by the CCO.

### 7.6 Gifts and Hospitality

All staff must record gifts and hospitality received or offered where the value exceeds £50 per instance, or where cumulative gifts or hospitality from a single source exceed £150 in any rolling 12-month period. Declarations must be made within five business days using the Gifts and Hospitality Register on the intranet.

- Gifts or hospitality with a value exceeding £250 per instance require prior approval from the relevant line manager and the Compliance team.
- Cash gifts must never be accepted, regardless of value.
- The CCO will review the Gifts and Hospitality Register quarterly and will report any patterns or concerns to the Compliance Oversight Committee.

### 7.7 Outside Business Interests

All staff must declare any outside business interest — including directorships, significant shareholdings, consultancy arrangements, or other paid or unpaid external roles — to the Compliance team before taking on the interest, or, where this is not practicable, within five business days of commencing the interest.

- The Compliance team will assess whether the outside interest gives rise to a conflict and will notify the staff member of the outcome within ten business days.
- Approved outside interests will be recorded in the Outside Business Interests Register, maintained by Compliance.
- Staff must re-declare any material change to an approved outside interest within five business days of the change occurring.
- Senior Managers must additionally comply with the notification requirements under SM&CR where an outside interest is relevant to their Senior Manager Function.

**Escalation path:** Individual staff members escalate conflicts to their line manager and the Compliance team. The CCO escalates unresolved or high-severity conflicts to the Executive Risk Committee, chaired by David Okonkwo. Matters with Board-level significance, or where a conflict involves a Senior Manager, are escalated to the Board Risk Committee, chaired by Michael Byrne. Where a conflict intersects with a potential regulatory breach, the CCO will notify the FCA in accordance with Crest Bank's Regulatory Reporting Policy.

## 9. Training

- All staff must complete mandatory conflicts of interest training upon joining Crest Bank and annually thereafter.
- Staff in designated roles under the Personal Account Dealing Rules must complete additional training on market abuse and personal account dealing obligations, in accordance with MAR 1, upon appointment and annually thereafter.
- Senior Managers and Certified Persons must complete SM&CR-specific training that addresses conflicts of interest in the context of individual accountability.
- Training completion is tracked via Crest Bank's Learning Management System (LMS). Non-completion is reported to the relevant line manager and, where persistent, escalated to the CCO.
- The Compliance team will review training content annually to ensure it remains current with regulatory developments.

---

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy will be reviewed annually by the CCO, with approval by the Compliance Oversight Committee. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- a material change to FCA or PRA rules relevant to conflicts of interest;
- identification of a significant conflict that reveals a gap in the policy framework;
- a regulatory finding, enforcement action, or internal audit recommendation relating to conflicts of interest;
- a material change to Crest Bank's business model, product range, or organisational structure; or
- a direction from the Board Risk Committee or Executive Risk Committee.

Any amendments to this policy require approval by the Compliance Oversight Committee before taking effect. Material amendments will be reported to the Board Risk Committee at its next scheduled meeting.
