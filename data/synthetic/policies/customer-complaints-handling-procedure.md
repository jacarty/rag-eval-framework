## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's framework for receiving, investigating, and resolving customer complaints in a manner that is fair, consistent, and compliant with FCA requirements. It ensures that customers who are dissatisfied with Crest Bank's products or services receive a timely and transparent response, and that complaint data is used systematically to drive service improvement. The policy applies to all regulated and unregulated complaints received across Crest Bank's digital channels.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Staff:** All customer-facing staff in Customer Operations, as well as staff in Product, Risk, Compliance, and Technology who may be involved in complaint investigation or root cause analysis.
- **Products:** Personal current accounts, instant-access and fixed-term savings accounts, personal loans, and SME business current accounts.
- **Customer Segments:** All retail customers (approximately 850,000) and SME customers (approximately 12,000).
- **Channels:** In-app messaging, email, web contact form, and any written correspondence received at Crest Bank's registered address.
- **Systems:** Crest Bank's complaint management system (CMS), core banking platform, and customer identity and verification (CIV) system.

## 5. Policy Statement

1. Crest Bank treats every expression of dissatisfaction from a customer as a complaint and will handle it with fairness, diligence, and transparency, in accordance with DISP 1.
2. Crest Bank will acknowledge all complaints promptly and resolve them within the timescales prescribed by DISP 1, communicating clearly with the customer at every stage.
3. Crest Bank will investigate each complaint impartially, considering all relevant evidence before reaching a conclusion, and will not allow commercial considerations to influence the outcome.
4. Crest Bank will inform all complainants of their right to refer unresolved complaints to the Financial Ombudsman Service, in the manner and at the times required by DISP 1.
5. Crest Bank will use complaint data to identify systemic issues and root causes, and will take proportionate remedial action to prevent recurrence, in support of its obligations under PRIN 12.
6. Crest Bank will maintain complete and accurate complaint records for a minimum of five years from the date of receipt, in accordance with SYSC 9.1.
7. Crest Bank will report complaint management information (MI) to the relevant governance bodies on a regular basis and will submit regulatory returns to the FCA as required by DISP 1.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Definition and Receipt of Complaints

A complaint is any oral or written expression of dissatisfaction, whether justified or not, from or on behalf of an eligible complainant, as defined in DISP 2.7, about Crest Bank's provision of, or failure to provide, a financial service or a redress determination.

All customer-facing staff must recognise expressions of dissatisfaction and log them in the CMS on the same business day they are received. Staff must not attempt to resolve a complaint informally without logging it, unless the complaint is resolved by close of business on the next business day (the "next business day rule" under DISP 1.5). Where a complaint is resolved under this rule, it must still be recorded in the CMS with the resolution outcome noted.

### 7.2 Complaint Logging and Acknowledgement

Upon receipt of a complaint, the assigned complaints handler must:

1. Log the complaint in the CMS, capturing: customer name, account number, channel of receipt, date and time of receipt, nature of the complaint, and product or service affected.
2. Assign a unique complaint reference number and communicate this to the customer.
3. Send a written acknowledgement to the customer within **five business days** of receipt, in accordance with DISP 1.6.2R. The acknowledgement must confirm: the complaint reference number, the name and contact details of the handler, and the expected resolution timescale.

### 7.3 Investigation Process

The complaints handler must conduct a fair and impartial investigation, which will include:

1. Reviewing all relevant account records, transaction data, and customer communications held in Crest Bank's systems.
2. Obtaining written input from the relevant business area (e.g., Product, Technology, Credit) within **five business days** of the request being made.
3. Considering whether the complaint indicates a potential data breach, financial crime, or systemic product failure, and referring to the DPO, MLRO, or CTO respectively where applicable.
4. Documenting all investigative steps and evidence reviewed in the CMS.

Where a complaint is complex or involves multiple business areas, the complaints handler must escalate to the Head of Customer Operations, who will appoint a lead investigator and set an internal resolution target.

### 7.4 Final Response Letters

Crest Bank must issue a final response letter to the complainant in accordance with DISP 1.6.2R. The final response must:

1. Be issued within **eight weeks** of the date the complaint was received.
2. State clearly whether Crest Bank upholds, partially upholds, or rejects the complaint, with reasons.
3. Where the complaint is upheld or partially upheld, set out the redress or remedy offered, including any financial compensation and the basis on which it has been calculated.
4. Inform the complainant of their right to refer the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service if they remain dissatisfied, in accordance with DISP 1.6.2R (see section 7.5).
5. Be written in plain English, free from jargon, and accessible to the customer.

All final response letters for complaints involving financial redress of £500 or more, or any complaint assessed as having potential regulatory significance, must be reviewed and countersigned by the Head of Customer Operations before despatch.

### 7.5 FOS Referral Rights

In accordance with DISP 1.6.2R, Crest Bank must notify the complainant of their right to refer the complaint to the FOS:

- **In the final response letter**, regardless of whether the complaint is upheld or rejected.
- **In a written response issued at eight weeks**, if Crest Bank has not yet been able to issue a final response, explaining the reason for the delay and confirming the FOS referral right.

All communications informing customers of FOS referral rights must include the FOS contact details (website: www.financial-ombudsman.org.uk; telephone: 0800 023 4567) and must state that the customer has **six months** from the date of the final response to refer the complaint to the FOS.

Crest Bank will cooperate fully with the FOS in respect of any referred complaints, providing all requested documentation within the timeframes set by the FOS.

### 7.6 Root Cause Analysis

In accordance with Crest Bank's obligations under PRIN 12 to deliver good outcomes for customers, the Head of Customer Operations will ensure that root cause analysis (RCA) is conducted as follows:

1. **Individual complaints:** Each upheld complaint must include a brief RCA note in the CMS, identifying the immediate cause of the failure.
2. **Thematic analysis:** On a monthly basis, the complaints team will analyse complaint data to identify recurring themes, products, or processes generating disproportionate complaint volumes.
3. **Formal RCA:** Where a theme generates five or more upheld complaints within a rolling 30-day period, or where a single complaint indicates a systemic product or process failure, the Head of Customer Operations will commission a formal RCA report within **ten business days**.
4. The formal RCA report will be shared with the relevant business area owner and the CCO, who will agree a remediation plan with defined owners and completion dates.
5. Remediation plans will be tracked in the CMS and reported to the Executive Risk Committee monthly until closure.

### 7.7 Vulnerable Customers

Where a complaint is received from a customer who has been identified as potentially vulnerable, as defined in Crest Bank's Vulnerable Customer Policy, the complaints handler must:

1. Flag the complaint as involving a vulnerable customer in the CMS.
2. Prioritise the investigation and aim to issue a final response within **four weeks** rather than the standard eight weeks.
3. Offer alternative communication formats or channels if requested by the customer.
4. Consider whether the circumstances giving rise to the complaint indicate that additional support or referral to specialist services is appropriate.

## 8. Monitoring, Reporting and Escalation

### 8.1 Complaint MI

The Head of Customer Operations will produce a monthly Complaint MI Report covering:

- Total complaints received, broken down by product, channel, and complaint category.
- Complaints resolved within timescale vs. those breaching the eight-week deadline.
- Upheld rate, partial upheld rate, and rejection rate.
- Total financial redress paid.
- Complaints referred to the FOS and outcomes of FOS decisions.
- Open RCA actions and their status.

### 8.2 Reporting to Governance Bodies

| Report | Recipient | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Complaint MI Report | Executive Risk Committee | Monthly |
| Complaint MI Summary | Compliance Oversight Committee | Monthly |
| Complaint Trends Summary | Board Risk Committee | Quarterly |
| FCA Complaints Return (DISP 1 Annex 1) | FCA (via RegData) | Half-yearly |

### 8.3 Escalation

- Any complaint involving potential regulatory breach, media risk, or financial redress exceeding £10,000 must be escalated to the CCO and Head of Customer Operations within **one business day** of identification.
- Any complaint indicating potential financial crime must be referred to the MLRO on the same business day.
- Any complaint indicating a material data breach must be referred to the DPO on the same business day, who will assess notification obligations under UK GDPR.
- Systemic issues identified through RCA that cannot be remediated within 30 days must be escalated to the Executive Risk Committee for direction.
