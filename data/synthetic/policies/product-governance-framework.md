## 2. Purpose

This framework establishes Crest Bank's obligations and internal standards for the design, approval, distribution, and ongoing review of all retail and SME products. It ensures that products are developed and maintained in the interests of identified target markets, delivering fair value throughout the product lifecycle. The framework implements the FCA's Consumer Duty and product governance requirements, embedding customer outcomes as a central consideration at every stage of product development and management.

---

## 3. Scope

This framework applies to:

- **Products and services:** All personal current accounts, instant-access savings, fixed-term savings, personal loans, and SME business current accounts offered by Crest Bank, whether newly developed, materially changed, or under ongoing review.
- **Teams and functions:** Product Management, Pricing, Technology, Customer Operations, Compliance, Risk, Legal, and Marketing.
- **Customer segments:** All retail customers (approximately 850,000) and SME customers (approximately 12,000).
- **Distribution:** All channels operated by Crest Bank, including the Crest Bank mobile application, web platform, and any third-party distribution arrangements.
- **Third parties:** Any distributor, introducer, or partner through whom Crest Bank products are made available to end customers.

This framework does not apply to internal treasury products or interbank arrangements that are not offered to retail or SME customers.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to the following principles in respect of product governance:

1. **Customer-centred design.** All products will be designed with a clearly defined target market in mind. Product features, pricing, and terms will be structured to meet the genuine needs, characteristics, and objectives of that target market, in accordance with PRIN 2A.

2. **Robust pre-launch approval.** No product or material product change will be made available to customers without completing the formal Product Approval Process set out in this framework, including sign-off by the Product Governance Committee.

3. **Fair value.** Crest Bank will assess and document the fair value of every product before launch and at each scheduled review, ensuring that the price customers pay is reasonable relative to the benefit they receive, consistent with PRIN 2A.4.

4. **Appropriate distribution.** Crest Bank will define and document a distribution strategy for each product, ensuring it reaches only those customers for whom it is suitable and that any third-party distributors are provided with sufficient information to fulfil their own governance obligations.

5. **Ongoing monitoring.** Products will be subject to continuous and periodic monitoring against defined outcome metrics. Where monitoring identifies that a product is causing poor customer outcomes, Crest Bank will act promptly to investigate and remediate.

6. **Lifecycle management.** Crest Bank will manage products actively throughout their lifecycle, including at the point of material change, wind-down, or withdrawal, ensuring that customers are treated fairly at every stage.

7. **Accountability and escalation.** Clear ownership of product governance obligations will be assigned to named individuals. Material concerns identified during the product lifecycle will be escalated to the appropriate governance body without delay.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Target Market Identification

7.1.1 For every new product, the responsible Product Manager will prepare a Target Market Statement defining the intended customer population by reference to: customer type (retail or SME), financial situation, risk tolerance, needs and objectives, and knowledge and experience where relevant.

7.1.2 The Target Market Statement must also identify any negative target market — categories of customer for whom the product is not designed and to whom it should not be distributed.

7.1.3 In accordance with PROD 1.4 and PRIN 2A, the Target Market Statement will be reviewed and approved by the CCO before the product proceeds to the approval stage.

7.1.4 Where Crest Bank acts as distributor for a third-party product, Priya Sharma will confirm that the third-party manufacturer's target market is consistent with Crest Bank's customer base before distribution commences.

### 7.2 Product Approval Process

7.2.1 No new product or material change to an existing product may be launched without completing the Product Approval Process. A "material change" includes any change to pricing, eligibility criteria, key features, terms and conditions, or distribution channel.

7.2.2 The responsible Product Manager will compile a **Product Approval Pack** comprising:

- Target Market Statement (see 7.1)
- Product description and feature summary
- Fair Value Assessment (see 7.3)
- Risk assessment, signed off by David Okonkwo
- Financial crime risk review, signed off by James Patel
- Data protection impact assessment or screening note, signed off by Fatima Al-Rashid
- Technology readiness confirmation, signed off by Tom Hargreaves
- Distribution Strategy (see 7.4)
- Customer communications and disclosure drafts
- Proposed outcome monitoring metrics (see 7.5)

7.2.3 The completed Product Approval Pack will be submitted to the Product Governance Committee at least ten business days before the proposed launch date.

7.2.4 The Product Governance Committee will review the pack and may approve, approve with conditions, request further information, or reject the product. Decisions will be recorded in the Committee minutes.

7.2.5 In accordance with SYSC 4.1, the approval decision and rationale will be documented and retained for a minimum of six years.

### 7.3 Fair Value Assessment

7.3.1 Consistent with PRIN 2A and FG22/5, the responsible Product Manager will prepare a Fair Value Assessment for every product prior to launch and at each annual review.

7.3.2 The Fair Value Assessment will document: the total cost to the customer (including fees, charges, and interest rates), the benefits delivered, a comparison with comparable market products where data is available, and a conclusion on whether the product represents fair value for the target market.

7.3.3 Where the assessment concludes that a product does not represent fair value, the Product Manager will propose remediation options to the Product Governance Committee before the product is approved or continues in distribution.

7.3.4 Fair Value Assessments will be reviewed by the CCO before submission to the Product Governance Committee.

### 7.4 Distribution Strategy

7.4.1 The Product Manager will document a Distribution Strategy for each product, specifying: the intended distribution channels (mobile app, web platform, or third-party), any eligibility restrictions applied at the point of sale, and the customer journey and disclosure approach.

7.4.2 Where Crest Bank distributes products through third parties, Rachel Whitfield will ensure that written agreements are in place requiring distributors to comply with their own product governance obligations and to share relevant customer outcome data with Crest Bank.

7.4.3 In accordance with COBS 2.1.1R, customer-facing communications and disclosures will be reviewed for clarity, accuracy, and consistency with the Target Market Statement before distribution commences.

### 7.5 Ongoing Product Monitoring

7.5.1 Each product will have a defined set of outcome metrics, agreed at the point of approval, covering at minimum: customer complaints volumes and themes, product usage and feature uptake, arrears and default rates (for lending products), account closure rates, and customer satisfaction scores.

7.5.2 Product Managers will produce a **Monthly Product Monitoring Report** for each product, reviewed by Priya Sharma and submitted to the Compliance Oversight Committee.

7.5.3 Where a metric breaches a pre-defined threshold, the Product Manager will initiate a root cause analysis within ten business days and present findings to the Product Governance Committee at its next scheduled meeting.

7.5.4 The CCO will provide a consolidated product outcomes summary to the Board Risk Committee on a quarterly basis.

### 7.6 Product Lifecycle Management

7.6.1 All products will undergo a formal **Annual Product Review**, led by the responsible Product Manager and reviewed by the Product Governance Committee. The review will reassess the Target Market Statement, Fair Value Assessment, and outcome metrics.

7.6.2 Where a product is to be materially changed, the Product Manager will complete an updated Product Approval Pack in accordance with section 7.2.

7.6.3 Where a product is to be withdrawn or wound down, the Product Manager will prepare a **Wind-Down Plan** addressing: customer notification timelines (minimum 30 days' notice for savings and lending products unless regulatory requirements specify otherwise), migration options for existing customers, and data retention obligations.

7.6.4 Wind-Down Plans must be approved by the Product Governance Committee and, where the withdrawal affects more than 10,000 customers, by Sarah Chen as CEO.

## 9. Training

9.1 All staff with product development, pricing, marketing, or distribution responsibilities must complete Crest Bank's **Product Governance and Consumer Duty** training module before undertaking product-related work and annually thereafter.

9.2 The training module covers: the Consumer Duty obligations under PRIN 2A, target market identification, fair value assessment methodology, and the Product Approval Process.

9.3 Training completion is recorded in Crest Bank's Learning Management System. Rachel Whitfield will review completion rates quarterly and escalate material gaps to the Compliance Oversight Committee.

9.4 New members of the Product Governance Committee will complete a tailored induction covering their governance obligations under this framework within 30 days of appointment.

---

## 10. Review Cycle

10.1 This framework will be reviewed annually, with the next scheduled review due by 31 January 2026, led by Rachel Whitfield and approved by the Product Governance Committee.

10.2 An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- Material change to FCA or PRA rules or guidance affecting product governance obligations, including updates to PRIN 2A or PROD.
- A significant product-related customer harm event or regulatory finding.
- A material change to Crest Bank's product range or distribution model.
- A direction from the Board Risk Committee or Executive Risk Committee.

10.3 Proposed amendments to this framework require approval by the Product Governance Committee. Amendments that materially alter the scope of the framework or the responsibilities of the Board Risk Committee additionally require endorsement by the Board Risk Committee.
