## 2. Purpose

This policy sets out Crest Bank's commitment to ensuring that fair treatment of customers is embedded throughout the organisation, from product design through to post-sale service and complaint resolution. It gives effect to the FCA's Consumer Duty requirements and the longstanding Treating Customers Fairly (TCF) framework, translating regulatory obligations into actionable standards for all staff. The policy provides a consistent basis against which Crest Bank's conduct can be assessed, monitored, and improved.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Staff:** All Crest Bank employees, contractors, and third-party agents who interact with customers, design products, develop communications, or make decisions affecting customer outcomes.
- **Products and services:** Personal current accounts, instant-access and fixed-term savings accounts, personal loans, and SME business current accounts.
- **Customer segments:** All retail customers (approximately 850,000) and SME customers (approximately 12,000), including prospective customers at the pre-sale stage.
- **Systems and channels:** All digital channels through which Crest Bank delivers services, including the mobile application, web platform, in-app messaging, and automated decisioning systems.

This policy does not apply to Crest Bank's relationships with wholesale counterparties or institutional clients.

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank is committed to the following core principles in all its dealings with customers:

1. **Six TCF Outcomes.** Crest Bank will conduct its business in a manner designed to deliver all six TCF outcomes: (i) customers can be confident they are dealing with a firm where fair treatment is central to its culture; (ii) products and services marketed and sold are designed to meet the needs of identified customer groups; (iii) customers are provided with clear information before, during, and after the point of sale; (iv) where advice is given, it is suitable and takes account of customer circumstances; (v) products perform as customers have been led to expect and service is of an acceptable standard; and (vi) customers do not face unreasonable post-sale barriers to changing product, switching provider, submitting a claim, or making a complaint.

2. **Product Design and Governance.** All products and services will be designed with a clearly defined target market and assessed against the needs, characteristics, and objectives of that market before launch and at regular intervals thereafter.

3. **Clear and Fair Communications.** All customer-facing communications, including marketing materials, terms and conditions, in-app notifications, and automated messages, will be written in plain English, free from misleading statements, and designed to support informed customer decision-making.

4. **Vulnerable Customer Identification and Support.** Crest Bank will maintain proportionate and effective processes to identify customers who may be in vulnerable circumstances and will adapt its service delivery to meet their needs, ensuring they receive outcomes that are at least as good as those received by other customers.

5. **Post-Sale Service Standards.** Crest Bank will provide a consistently high standard of service after the point of sale, ensuring customers can access support, exercise their rights, and resolve problems without unreasonable difficulty.

6. **Complaints as a Fairness Indicator.** Crest Bank will treat complaints data as a key source of management information about customer outcomes and will use it to identify and address systemic fairness issues.

7. **Accountability and Culture.** Responsibility for fair customer outcomes is not limited to the Compliance function. Every team and individual at Crest Bank is accountable for the outcomes their work produces for customers.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Product Design and Target Market Assessment

7.1.1 Before launching any new product or materially amending an existing product, the relevant Product Owner will complete a Target Market Assessment (TMA) documenting the intended customer group, the needs the product is designed to meet, and any features that could cause harm if used outside the target market.

7.1.2 The TMA will be reviewed and approved by the Product Governance Committee before launch. In accordance with PRIN 2A, the assessment must address all four Consumer Duty outcome areas.

7.1.3 Product Owners will review each product's performance against its TMA at least annually, using data including usage patterns, complaint volumes, and customer satisfaction scores. Material adverse findings will be escalated to the Product Governance Committee within 10 business days of identification.

### 7.2 Communications Standards

7.2.1 In accordance with PRIN 7 and COBS 2.1.1R, all customer-facing communications must be reviewed against Crest Bank's Communications Clarity Checklist before publication. The checklist requires that communications are written at no higher than a Grade 8 reading level, avoid jargon, state key terms prominently, and include all material information a customer needs to make an informed decision.

7.2.2 The Compliance team will conduct a sample review of at least 10% of new customer communications each calendar quarter. Communications that fail the checklist will be withdrawn or amended before further distribution.

7.2.3 Automated communications generated by system processes (including in-app notifications and decisioning outcome letters) will be reviewed by the CTO and CCO jointly at least annually to confirm continued compliance with this standard.

### 7.3 Vulnerable Customer Identification and Support

7.3.1 Crest Bank recognises vulnerability as a spectrum and adopts the FCA's definition in FG21/1, which identifies four drivers of vulnerability: health, life events, resilience, and capability.

7.3.2 All customer-facing staff will be trained to recognise indicators of vulnerability across these four drivers. Where a vulnerability indicator is identified during any customer interaction, the staff member will record it on the customer's account record using the designated vulnerability flag in Crest Bank's CRM system.

7.3.3 Once a vulnerability flag is applied, the Head of Customer Operations will ensure that the customer's subsequent interactions are routed, where practicable, to staff with enhanced vulnerability training, and that any automated processes (such as collections or account restriction workflows) are reviewed for appropriateness before being applied to that customer.

7.3.4 Crest Bank will review the effectiveness of its vulnerability identification processes at least every six months, using data on flagging rates, complaint rates among flagged customers, and outcomes of quality assurance reviews.

### 7.4 Post-Sale Service Standards

7.4.1 Crest Bank commits to the following minimum post-sale service standards: (i) customer enquiries acknowledged within one business day; (ii) account closure or product switching requests processed within five business days of receipt of all required information; (iii) no unreasonable barriers, including excessive documentation requirements or disproportionate fees, placed on customers exercising their contractual or statutory rights.

7.4.2 In accordance with DISP 1, all complaints will be acknowledged within five business days and resolved within eight weeks. The Head of Customer Operations will maintain a complaints register and produce a monthly summary for the Compliance Oversight Committee.

7.4.3 Where a customer raises a concern that does not meet the formal definition of a complaint but indicates a potential fairness issue, the handling staff member will escalate the concern to their line manager, who will determine whether a formal complaint should be raised on the customer's behalf.

## 8. Monitoring, Reporting and Escalation

8.1 The CCO will maintain a TCF Outcomes Dashboard, updated monthly, tracking the following indicators: product complaint rates by product line; vulnerable customer flagging rates; communications quality assurance pass rates; post-sale service standard adherence rates; and Net Promoter Score trends.

8.2 A TCF Outcomes Report will be presented to the Compliance Oversight Committee at each monthly meeting and to the Board Risk Committee on a quarterly basis.

8.3 Where any indicator breaches a pre-defined tolerance threshold, the CCO will notify the CRO within two business days. If the breach indicates a potential systemic fairness failure affecting a material number of customers, the CCO will escalate to the Executive Risk Committee for an extraordinary review within five business days.

8.4 Significant conduct risk events, including any matter that may require proactive customer redress, will be reported to the Board Risk Committee at its next scheduled meeting and, where required by FCA rules, notified to the FCA under SUP 15.

## 9. Training

9.1 All new employees will complete Crest Bank's TCF and Consumer Duty induction module within 30 days of joining. Completion is recorded in the Learning Management System (LMS).

9.2 All staff in customer-facing roles, product design roles, and communications roles will complete a refresher TCF training module annually. The module will be updated to reflect any regulatory developments or internal lessons learned identified during the preceding year.

9.3 Staff with designated responsibilities for vulnerable customer support will complete the enhanced vulnerability awareness module, aligned to FG21/1, upon appointment to that role and annually thereafter.

9.4 Training completion rates will be reported to the Compliance Oversight Committee quarterly. Any team with a completion rate below 95% will be subject to a targeted remediation plan agreed between the CCO and the relevant Head of Department within 10 business days.

## 10. Review Cycle

10.1 This policy will be reviewed annually by the CCO, with the next scheduled review due by 31 January 2026. The revised policy will be approved by the Compliance Oversight Committee before the effective date of any new version.

10.2 An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following: a material change to FCA rules or guidance relating to consumer protection or Consumer Duty; a significant conduct risk event or customer redress exercise; a material change to Crest Bank's product range or customer base; or a recommendation from the Board Risk Committee or Executive Risk Committee.

10.3 Any substantive amendments to this policy require approval by the Compliance Oversight Committee. Minor amendments (such as updates to named individuals) may be approved by the CCO and recorded in the document version history.
