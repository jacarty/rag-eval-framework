## 2. Purpose

This policy establishes Crest Bank's framework for identifying, assessing, and managing risks arising from the outsourcing of functions and the use of third-party service providers. It ensures that Crest Bank meets its regulatory obligations regarding outsourcing governance and maintains operational resilience where business functions are delivered by external parties. The policy applies throughout the lifecycle of a third-party relationship, from initial due diligence through to exit and termination.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Staff:** All Crest Bank employees involved in procuring, managing, or overseeing third-party relationships, including senior management, the Procurement function, Technology, Risk, Compliance, and business line owners.
- **Products and Services:** All personal current accounts, savings products, personal loans, and SME business current accounts where any element of service delivery, processing, or infrastructure is provided by a third party.
- **Third Parties:** All external providers of services, technology, data processing, cloud infrastructure, payment processing, credit decisioning, customer communications, and any other function that supports Crest Bank's regulated activities.
- **Systems:** All technology platforms, APIs, and data-sharing arrangements involving external vendors.

This policy does not apply to the purchase of standard off-the-shelf software with no customisation or ongoing service dependency, nor to one-off professional advisory engagements below the materiality threshold defined in the Crest Bank Procurement Policy.

## 4. Regulatory Context

| Regulatory Source | Relevance to This Policy |
|---|---|
| **SYSC 8** (Outsourcing) | Sets out requirements for firms to ensure outsourced functions are subject to appropriate oversight, that service providers have the capability to perform functions reliably, and that Crest Bank retains responsibility for compliance with regulatory obligations. |
| **SYSC 13** (Operational Risk) | Requires Crest Bank to manage operational risks arising from third-party dependencies, including technology and infrastructure risks. |
| **SYSC 4.1** (General Organisational Requirements) | Requires Crest Bank to organise its affairs responsibly and effectively, including maintaining clear accountability for outsourced functions. |

| **EBA Guidelines on Outsourcing Arrangements (EBA/GL/2019/02)** | Although post-Brexit these guidelines are not directly binding, Crest Bank adopts them as best practice, particularly regarding the register of outsourcing arrangements and concentration risk assessment. |

## 5. Policy Statement

Crest Bank adopts the following principles in managing outsourcing and third-party risk:

1. **Accountability is retained.** Outsourcing a function does not transfer Crest Bank's regulatory responsibility for that function. Crest Bank remains fully accountable to the FCA and PRA for all outsourced activities, in accordance with SYSC 8.1.
2. **All third-party relationships are risk-assessed.** Every third-party engagement must be subject to a documented risk assessment before commencement, and periodically thereafter. The depth of assessment is proportionate to the criticality of the function.
3. **Critical and important outsourcing is subject to enhanced governance.** Arrangements classified as critical or important outsourcing (CIO) are subject to additional due diligence, contractual requirements, regulatory notification obligations, and Board-level oversight.
4. **Contracts must protect Crest Bank's ability to be supervised.** All outsourcing contracts must include provisions enabling regulatory access, audit rights, and data portability, ensuring the FCA and PRA can exercise their supervisory functions without impediment.
5. **Concentration risk is actively managed.** Crest Bank monitors and limits its dependency on any single third-party provider or group of providers, particularly in technology infrastructure and payment processing.
6. **Exit strategies are mandatory for critical and important outsourcing.** Crest Bank maintains documented, tested exit plans for all CIO arrangements to ensure continuity of service to customers in the event of provider failure or contract termination.
7. **Third-party performance is continuously monitored.** Crest Bank does not rely solely on contractual protections; ongoing performance monitoring and periodic reviews are conducted for all material third-party relationships.
8. **Sub-outsourcing is controlled.** Third parties must notify Crest Bank before sub-outsourcing any material element of the services they provide, and Crest Bank retains the right to object to sub-outsourcing arrangements that introduce unacceptable risk.

## 7. Procedures

### 7.1 Classification of Outsourcing Arrangements

7.1.1 Before engaging any third party, the relevant business line owner must complete a **Third-Party Classification Form**, available on Crest Bank's intranet.

7.1.2 Arrangements are classified as follows:

- **Critical or Important Outsourcing (CIO):** Functions where a defect or failure would materially impair Crest Bank's ability to meet its regulatory obligations, serve customers, or maintain financial soundness. Examples include core banking platform, payment processing, cloud infrastructure hosting regulated data, and credit decisioning engines.
- **Material Third-Party Arrangement:** Functions that are operationally significant but do not meet the CIO threshold. Enhanced due diligence applies.
- **Standard Third-Party Arrangement:** All other engagements. Standard due diligence applies.

7.1.3 The CRO (David Okonkwo) must approve all CIO classifications. In accordance with SYSC 8.1, Crest Bank must notify the FCA and PRA of new CIO arrangements before they commence, using the prescribed notification process managed by the CCO.

### 7.2 Due Diligence

7.2.1 **Standard due diligence** must be completed for all third-party engagements and includes: financial stability checks, information security questionnaire, data protection assessment (coordinated with the DPO, Fatima Al-Rashid), and business continuity capability review.

7.2.2 **Enhanced due diligence** for material and CIO arrangements additionally requires: on-site or virtual audit of the provider's operational controls; review of the provider's own outsourcing and sub-outsourcing arrangements; assessment of the provider's regulatory standing and any enforcement history; and a concentration risk assessment (see 7.5).

7.2.3 Due diligence findings must be documented in the **Third-Party Risk Assessment Report** and approved by the CRO before contract execution for CIO arrangements, or by the relevant business line owner (with Procurement sign-off) for standard arrangements.

7.2.4 In accordance with SYSC 13, due diligence must specifically assess the operational resilience of the provider, including their ability to recover from technology failures, cyber incidents, and business disruption events.

### 7.3 Contractual Requirements

7.3.1 All outsourcing contracts must be reviewed by Legal and Compliance before execution. The Procurement function maintains a **standard outsourcing contract schedule** containing mandatory clauses.

7.3.2 All contracts must include, at minimum:

- A clear description of the services and associated service levels (SLAs) with defined remedies for breach.
- Crest Bank's right to audit the provider, either directly or through a nominated third party, with reasonable notice.
- The FCA's and PRA's right of access to the provider's premises, systems, and personnel for supervisory purposes, in accordance with SYSC 8.1 and PRA SS2/21.
- Data protection provisions consistent with UK GDPR and Crest Bank's Data Protection Policy, reviewed and approved by the DPO.
- Notification obligations requiring the provider to inform Crest Bank of material incidents, regulatory actions, sub-outsourcing changes, or changes in ownership within defined timeframes (no longer than 48 hours for operational incidents affecting service delivery).
- Termination rights, including termination for convenience with adequate notice periods and termination for cause.
- Provisions governing data return and deletion upon contract termination.

7.3.3 For CIO arrangements, contracts must additionally include: step-in rights enabling Crest Bank to assume direct control of the function in an emergency; explicit sub-outsourcing consent requirements; and provisions supporting Crest Bank's exit plan (see 7.6).

### 7.4 Ongoing Monitoring

7.4.1 Each third-party relationship must have a designated **Relationship Owner** (a named Crest Bank employee) who is responsible for day-to-day oversight and performance monitoring.

7.4.2 Relationship Owners must conduct and document **quarterly performance reviews** for CIO and material arrangements, assessing performance against SLAs, incident history, financial stability, and any changes in the provider's risk profile.

7.4.3 **Annual deep-dive reviews** must be conducted for all CIO arrangements. These reviews must include a reassessment of the original due diligence findings, a review of the provider's audit reports (e.g. ISAE 3402 / SOC 2 reports where applicable), and an updated concentration risk assessment.

7.4.4 Material performance failures, security incidents, or changes in the provider's financial or regulatory standing must be escalated by the Relationship Owner to the CRO within 24 hours of becoming aware.

### 7.5 Concentration Risk

7.5.1 The Procurement function, in coordination with the CRO, maintains a **Concentration Risk Register** that maps Crest Bank's operational dependencies across all third-party providers, identifying single points of failure and provider groups.

7.5.2 Crest Bank sets the following concentration risk thresholds, reviewed annually by the Executive Risk Committee:

- No single technology infrastructure provider (including cloud providers) may host more than 70% of Crest Bank's critical data or processing capacity without Board Risk Committee approval and a documented mitigation plan.
- No single payment processing provider may handle more than 60% of Crest Bank's total payment volumes without equivalent approval.

7.5.3 Where concentration thresholds are approached or breached, the CRO must present a remediation plan to the Executive Risk Committee within 30 days.

### 7.6 Exit Planning

7.6.1 An **Exit and Transition Plan** must be documented for every CIO arrangement at the point of contract execution, and reviewed annually thereafter.

7.6.2 Exit plans must address: the steps required to transfer the function to an alternative provider or bring it in-house; the estimated timeline and cost; data migration and portability requirements; customer communication obligations; and the identification of alternative providers.

7.6.3 Exit plans must be tested at least every two years through a desktop exercise or, where practicable, a partial live test. Test results must be reported to the Board Risk Committee.

7.6.4 The CTO (Tom Hargreaves) is responsible for ensuring that technology exit plans are technically viable and that data portability provisions in contracts support execution of the plan.

### 7.7 Register of Outsourcing Arrangements

7.7.1 The Procurement function maintains a **Register of Outsourcing Arrangements** covering all third-party engagements in scope of this policy. The register records, at minimum: provider name, function outsourced, classification (CIO / material / standard), contract start and expiry dates, Relationship Owner, date of last review, and regulatory notification status.

7.7.2 The register is reviewed and updated quarterly by the Procurement function and presented to the Executive Risk Committee. A summary is provided to the Board Risk Committee annually.

## 9. Training

- All staff with third-party relationship management responsibilities must complete Crest Bank's **Third-Party Risk Management** training module upon appointment to a relevant role and annually thereafter.
- Relationship Owners for CIO arrangements must additionally complete a **Critical Outsourcing Governance** module, covering regulatory notification obligations, exit planning, and audit rights, within 30 days of being assigned as Relationship Owner.
- Training completion is tracked via Crest Bank's Learning Management System (LMS). The CCO receives a quarterly completion report and escalates non-completion to the relevant line manager.
- The Compliance Oversight Committee reviews training completion rates annually and may direct targeted interventions where completion falls below 95%.

---

## 10. Review Cycle

This policy is reviewed annually by the CRO, with approval by the Board Risk Committee. An ad-hoc review will be triggered by any of the following:

- A material change in Crest Bank's outsourcing profile (e.g. onboarding a new CIO provider or exiting a significant arrangement).
- A regulatory change affecting outsourcing requirements, including new FCA or PRA rules, guidance, or supervisory statements.
- A significant operational incident involving a third-party provider.
- A finding from an internal audit or external review that identifies a material gap in this policy.
- A change in Crest Bank's strategic direction that materially affects its reliance on third parties.

Any material amendments to this policy require approval by the Board Risk Committee. Minor amendments (e.g. updates to named individuals or process references) may be approved by the CRO and ratified at the next Board Risk Committee meeting.
