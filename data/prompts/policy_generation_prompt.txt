## Document Requirements

Every policy document MUST follow this structure, in this order:

1. **Document Control** — policy title, version number, effective date, next review date, document owner (named individual and title), approving body (named committee)
2. **Purpose** — why this policy exists, 2-3 sentences
3. **Scope** — who and what is covered (which teams, products, customer segments, systems)
4. **Regulatory Context** — the specific FCA/PRA rules and guidance this policy implements (see FCA Referencing below)
5. **Policy Statement** — the core commitments, written as numbered policy principles (typically 4-8)
6. **Roles and Responsibilities** — a responsibility matrix mapping named roles to specific obligations under this policy
7. **Procedures** — the operational steps staff follow to comply, written as numbered sub-sections with enough detail to be actionable
8. **Monitoring, Reporting and Escalation** — what is monitored, how often, who receives reports, and the escalation path (include specific committee names)
9. **Training** — training requirements for relevant staff, frequency, and how completion is tracked
10. **Review Cycle** — how often the policy is reviewed (typically annually), what triggers an ad-hoc review, and who approves changes
11. **Related Documents** — cross-references to other Crest Bank policies that interact with this one

## FCA Referencing Rules

- Cite ONLY real FCA Handbook module codes that you are confident exist. Examples: PRIN 2 (Skill, care and diligence), SYSC 6.1.1R, COBS 2.1.1R, MCOB 3A, ICOBS 2.5.1R.
- Use the standard format: MODULE CHAPTER.SECTION.PROVISIONR/G/E (e.g. SYSC 10.1.3R for a Rule, SYSC 10.1.8G for Guidance).
- In the Regulatory Context section, list the primary FCA/PRA modules with a brief note on what each requires.
- Throughout the Procedures section, reference specific rules inline where a procedure directly implements a regulatory requirement (e.g. "In accordance with SYSC 6.1.2R, the CCO will...").
- Do NOT invent rule numbers. If you are unsure whether a specific provision number exists, reference the module and chapter level instead (e.g. "SYSC 6.1" rather than guessing at "SYSC 6.1.14R").
- Each policy should reference at least 3 distinct FCA modules.

## Tone and Style

- Write in formal but readable British English, consistent with a real bank policy document.
- Use "Crest Bank" (not "the Bank" or "we") for clarity.
- Use active voice where possible: "The MLRO will escalate..." not "Escalation will be performed by..."
- Be specific and actionable — avoid vague phrases like "appropriate measures will be taken."
- Target length: 800-2000 words (equivalent to 2-5 printed pages).

## Output Format

Return the policy as clean markdown. Use heading levels (## for main sections, ### for sub-sections). Use tables for the responsibility matrix. Do not include any preamble, commentary, or meta-text — output only the policy document itself.

## Critical Constraint

These are synthetic documents created for a fictional bank. They will be clearly labelled as synthetic in metadata. However, the quality, structure, and regulatory references must be realistic enough to serve as a credible test corpus for a RAG evaluation framework.
